## Page 2 — Mentor's narrative (flavor)
"Heed well my words, for I am Mentor, Guardian of Loretome." Tells of Zargon (evil
sorcerer), his general escaped beyond the Sea of Talons, the hero-prince Rogar the
barbarian, Durgin (dwarf warrior), Ladril (elf), Telor (wizard) who once allied to
defeat Zargon's Dread Hosts (orcs, goblins). Mentor guards the book **Loretome** but
"may not intervene, lest a greater evil befall the world." Flavor only.

## Page 4 — If You Are New to HeroQuest
- **WELCOME**: fantasy adventure in a hidden stone labyrinth of rooms & corridors
  controlled by Zargon & the forces of Dread. Four heroes summoned by Mentor to
  destroy the forces of Dread.
- **WHAT MAKES THIS GAME UNIQUE?**: one player is Zargon (controller/GM); others are
  the four heroes (barbarian, elf, dwarf, wizard). **A game for 2 to 5 players.**
  HeroQuest is played in **14 sequential gameplay sessions called quests**, described
  in the Quest Book. One quest may take **an hour or two**; each subsequent quest
  increases in difficulty. During a quest a hero may acquire treasures; riches may be
  used **between quests** to purchase weapons & armor from the **armory**. Heroes work
  together to defeat Zargon. "United winning is... Individual... Divided they fall."
  The adventure never ends — additional quests in Quest Packs (sold separately).
- **GAME CONTENTS**:
  - Gameboard
  - **31 monster miniatures**: 8 orcs, 6 goblins, **3 abominations, 4 Dread warriors,
    1 Dread sorcerer, 1 gargoyle**, 4 skeletons, 2 zombies, 2 mummies
  - **4 hero miniatures**: barbarian, dwarf, elf, wizard
  - **15 furniture pieces**: 2 tables, throne, alchemist's bench, 3 treasure chests,
    tomb, sorcerer's table, 2 bookcases, rack, fireplace, weapons rack, cupboard
  - **10 skull pieces**
  - **4 plastic rat pieces**
  - **21 dungeon doors**: 5 closed, 16 open
  - **97 game cards**: 24 treasure, 23 equipment, 14 artifact, 12 Dread spell,
    12 spell, 8 monster, 4 turn order
  - **Cardboard tiles**: stairs, blocked squares, secret doors, pit traps,
    falling-block traps, skulls
  - Quest Book
  - 4 character cards: barbarian, dwarf, elf, wizard
  - Game Master's screen
  - Pad of character sheets
  - **6 white combat dice**
  - **2 red dice** (movement; standard game rolls 2 red dice → our variant uses 3d4)

## Page 5 — How Do You Start? (roles)
- **GETTING STARTED**: first time, punch out cardboard tiles from the parts sheet.
- **Choose Your Role**: one player = Zargon (evil sorcerer); others = heroes
  (barbarian, dwarf, elf, wizard). If fewer than five people, one must still be
  Zargon; remaining players control more than one hero. Quests are more challenging
  with fewer than four heroes.
- **The Important Role of Zargon**: Zargon is the Game Master. Means: sit behind the
  GM screen; you control the quest for the other players; you alone know where
  monsters, secret doors, treasures and traps are; you alone have access to the Quest
  Book. Anyone can play Zargon, but experienced players may be more comfortable.
- **If You Are Playing as Zargon**: you run the quest; read game rules first; the
  Quest Book is for your eyes only, except passages you must read aloud (noted in the
  book).
- **HOW ZARGON SETS UP THE GAME** (Zargon icon):
  1. **Open the Quest Book and Read the First Quest** — "Quest 1 — The Trial" must be
     played first; read it silently. Each quest has three sections: parchment text,
     quest map, quest notes.
  - **Parchment Text**: outlines the heroes' challenge & reward; **always read aloud
    to the hero players at the beginning of the game.**
  - **Quest Map**: shows how the gameboard is laid out as the game progresses; marked
    with symbols for monster starting positions (identical to the monster cards,
    identified on the GM screen), furniture, stairs, doors, blocked-creature tiles,
    traps, secret doors, treasure chests; symbols are on the map legend. (red shield)
    **Do not place anything on the board yet — only reveal layout when hero figures
    move to a position that requires it.**
  - **Quest Notes**: read thoroughly before the quest; explain what happens in certain
    rooms and ... (continues p6).

## Page 6 — Setup steps 2–4 + hero stats + character card
- **2. Place the Gameboard on the Table** — HeroQuest logo on the right side as it
  faces you.
- **3. Place the Character Cards on the Table** — spread 4 character cards faceup; the
  number of dice and starting points for each hero appear on each card.
- **Body Points**: physical strength. Barbarian strongest = **8 Body**; Wizard weakest
  = **4 Body**.
- **Mind Points**: wisdom/intelligence/resistance to magic. Wizard greatest = **6
  Mind**; Barbarian least = **2 Mind**.
- **4. Direct Hero Players to Fill in Character Sheets** — give each player a sheet,
  pencil, character card, figure; fill in starting Body & Mind; may name character.
  Body/Mind change during quests (gained/lost); chart Body Points on the sheet; record
  treasure; save sheets between sessions.
- **Character Card** fields: Attack Dice, Defend Dice, Body Points, Mind Points,
  Movement, Starting Weapons, Starting Armor. (Wizard card shows Attack 1 / Defend 2 /
  Body 4 / Mind 6.)
  - **Attack Dice**: number of (white) combat dice rolled to attack — reflects the
    hero's weapon; changed by events (spells, combat, equipment).
  - **Defend Dice**: number of combat dice rolled to defend — ability to dodge/absorb
    hits; changed by events.

## Page 6/7 — setup steps 5–9 + cards
- **5. Position the Game Master's Screen** between you (Zargon) and the gameboard.
- **6. Place the Quest Book** between you and the GM screen.
- **7. Separate the Game Pieces** — doors, furniture, monsters, and all cardboard tiles
  into four piles. (To identify monsters quickly, see the last page of the Quest Book.)
- **8. Study the Quest Map and Place the Pieces in the Starting Room** — only place
  pieces that go in the starting room (usually the one with the stairway). (red shield)
  Do NOT put out any traps or secret doors; do not reveal treasure at this time.
- **Card setup**: Treasure cards — shuffle before each quest, facedown within reach of
  all. Artifact cards — kept behind the GM screen (revealed via quest notes). Monster
  cards — 8 cards spread faceup near the board for hero reference (same info as the
  monster chart on the GM screen).
- **9. Sort the Cards** into nine piles: equipment, treasure, artifacts, monsters,
  Dread spells, and four element spell decks: **air, fire, water, earth**.
- **Dread Spell Cards**: 12 Dread spells belong to Zargon; powerful; kept behind the
  GM screen. (Some Dread spells only used with Quest Packs.)

## Page 8 — Hero spells + dice placement (setup, cont.)
- **Hero Spell Cards — Air, Fire, Water, Earth**: four groups of hero spells, one per
  element; each group has **three** spells. Divided between the **wizard** and the
  **elf**.
- **Dividing the Spells** (exact order): the **wizard first chooses one** of the four
  spell groups; **next the elf chooses one** of the three that remain; **finally the
  two remaining groups go to the wizard**. → Wizard ends with **3 groups (9 spells)**,
  Elf with **1 group (3 spells)**.
- **Suggestion (first quest)**: wizard takes **fire**, elf takes **earth**, and the
  remaining (air + water) go to the wizard.
- **10. Place the Dice**: put **3 white combat dice + the 2 red dice** near the board
  for the heroes; Zargon keeps **3 white combat dice**. Then read "A Glimpse into the
  World of HeroQuest" aloud.

## Page 9 — A Glimpse into the World of HeroQuest (read aloud)
- Zargon narration to players. Establishes: heroes begin Quest 1 "The Trial" in the
  **room with the stairway** (revealed when Zargon places the stairway tile).
- **Quest 1 goal**: seek out and destroy **Verag, a foul gargoyle** who hides in the
  catacombs.
- On each turn, heroes discuss where to move / what to do; decisions affect all.
- **Movement**: "You move square by square along corridors and into rooms. The distance
  of your movement is determined by a roll of **two red dice**." (Base = 2d6; our
  variant swaps this to **3d4**.) "Where you move is within your control; what you find
  is within [Zargon's]."
- **Looking & revealing** (player perspective): "As you look down corridors and into
  rooms, I reveal to you many things, including closed doors, pieces of furniture,
  hideous monsters, blocked squares, and dangerous traps that you have sprung. I then
  place these items on the gameboard." Locations are on Zargon's quest map (hidden from
  heroes). "Peril lies in each step you take."

## Page 10 — Glimpse narration (cont., read aloud)
- Reinforces: combat success/failure determined by a roll of the **combat dice**
  (attack & defend). Some heroes fight with sword & armor; others wield magic spells —
  "cast your spells wisely!"
- "While you may use your turn to engage in combat or to cast a spell, you may instead
  choose to perform another action" — searching for treasure / secret doors / traps,
  or disarming a discovered, unsprung trap. **"You may only perform one action on your
  turn."**
- Zargon: "I control every monster present on the gameboard and may move all of them on
  one turn." Zargon also wields Dread magic.

## Page 11 — To Begin the Quest / Order of Play / On a Hero's Turn  ★core
- **To Begin**: Zargon reads the parchment text aloud. (red shield) ONLY the parchment
  text is read aloud; the quest map and quest notes stay secret.
- **Order of Play**: begins with the hero seated to **Zargon's left**, continues
  **clockwise**. After all heroes finish, it is **Zargon's turn** (he may move all
  monsters on the board). Repeat until the quest is achieved or the heroes leave the
  underworld. (Suggested seating: barbarian left of Zargon, then dwarf, elf, wizard.)
- **On Any Player's Turn** (Zargon OR hero): a player does ONE of:
  - **Move, then perform an action**, OR
  - **Perform an action, then move.**
- **On a Hero's Turn** (hero icon): "you may move and then perform an action, OR perform
  an action and then move. **You may NOT move part way, [act], and then move the rest.**"
  → ★ No splitting movement around the action. (Matches our engine's `markActed` rule.)
- **The six actions** (perform exactly ONE per turn; also printed on each turn-order
  card): 1 Attack · 2 Cast a spell · 3 Search for treasure · 4 Search for secret doors
  · 5 Search for traps · 6 Disarm a trap.
- **Hero Movement**:
  - Heroes normally **begin and end** a quest in the **stairway room** (unless the Quest
    Book says otherwise); must return to the stairway to safely complete a quest.
  - Distance = roll **two red dice** (→ our **3d4**); move square by square along
    corridors and into rooms.
  - Corridors = light-gray flooring, **1–2 squares wide**; rooms enclosed by white-line
    walls.
  - **You do NOT have to move the entire rolled distance.** (More movement rules listed
    on p12.)

## Page 12 — Movement rules (full) + Looking/Opening Doors + Six Actions intro  ★core
- **Full movement rules**:
  - Need not move the full rolled distance.
  - **Cannot pass over monsters**, move through walls, or **move diagonally**.
  - **May pass over other heroes.**
  - **May only enter rooms through doors.**
  - **May not share a square** with another hero or a monster, **except on the stairs or
    in a pit trap**.
- **Looking and Opening Doors** (both are FREE — NOT among the six actions; "additional
  things you may do on your turn"):
  - While moving you may **look down a corridor or through an open door**; you see what
    is **directly within line of sight**: closed doors, blocked-square tiles, monsters.
  - **If the line passes through a wall or a closed door, the miniature/tile is not
    visible.**
  - Move adjacent to a closed door and ask Zargon to open it (swap closed→open piece).
    **Opening a door reveals that room** — place its monsters, treasure chests, and
    items. (Don't put traps on secret doors; don't reveal treasure yet.)
  - **All doors start closed; once opened, a door can never be closed again.**
- **Free, non-action things** (any time on your turn): getting caught in a trap,
  drinking potions, picking things up, looking, opening doors.
- **Blocked-square tiles**: placed when they become visible; mark extra walls; **neither
  heroes nor monsters can move through blocked squares**.
- **How Zargon Reacts to Hero Movement**: watch movement vs the quest map; when a hero
  looks down a corridor/through a door, place the now-visible doors, blocked squares,
  monsters, chests, etc.

## Page 12/13 — Action 1: Attack + How a Hero Attacks  ★core combat
- **Action 1: Attack** — attack any monster you are **adjacent** to. **Adjacent =
  directly to the side, front, or rear** (the 4 orthogonal squares; diagram shows the
  elf adjacent in 4 orthogonal positions). Diagonal is NOT adjacent unless a weapon
  grants it. **Attack only once per turn.** Attack strength = weapon's Attack dice
  (stronger weapon → more dice). Most weapons attack orthogonally adjacent only; some
  allow **diagonal** or **ranged ("from a distance")**. Only one weapon at a time.
- **Starting weapons / heroes**:
  - **Barbarian** — broadsword (most powerful starting weapon).
  - **Dwarf** — shortsword; special knack for **disarming traps** (intuition).
  - **Elf** — shortsword; good fighter + some magic; starts each quest with **3 spells
    (1 group)**.
  - **Wizard** — dagger; master spellcaster; starts each quest with **9 spells (3
    groups)**; CANNOT wear normal armor or use large weapons.
- **How a Hero Attacks**: roll white combat dice = weapon's Attack dice. **Each SKULL =
  1 hit = 1 Body Point of damage.** No skulls → attack fails. The monster defends
  immediately.
- **How a Monster Defends** (Zargon): roll the monster's Defend dice (from the monster
  chart). **Each BLACK SHIELD blocks one hit.** Monster dies and is removed at **0 Body
  Points**. Many monsters die from one hit; some need more (track with **skull tiles**
  placed under the figure).
- A monster that **survives** an attack **cannot strike back until Zargon's turn** (no
  immediate retaliation).
- **Combat dice** (white, six-sided): faces are skulls (hits), white shields (hero
  defense), black shields (monster defense). [Standard composition 3 skull / 2 white
  shield / 1 black shield — confirm vs GM screen art.]
- **A Trip to the Armory**: between quests, spend gold coins on weapons & armor from the
  **equipment deck**; unlimited stock; buy any number of items.
- Cross-refs: How a Monster Attacks (p20), How a Hero Defends (p21).

## Page 14 — Weapons (diagonal/ranged), Line of Sight, Action 2 Cast a Spell  ★core LOS
- **Special weapons**:
  - **Ranged** ("hit a monster from a distance"): **daggers and crossbows**.
  - **Diagonal** attack weapons: **staff and longsword** (attack made & defended
    normally). A staff-wielding wizard can attack a monster diagonally while the monster
    **cannot** attack back diagonally ("safe" position). Diagonal weapons let more than
    one hero attack a monster blocking a doorway (e.g., wizard w/ staff diagonally +
    barbarian w/ broadsword orthogonally).
  - Full weapon/armor info is on the **equipment cards**.
- **Action 2: Cast a Spell**: the elf and wizard may cast a spell **instead of
  attacking**, at **anything they can see**, only on their turn. Target may be
  **yourself, another hero, or a monster**. **Each spell may be cast only once per
  quest**, then the card is discarded for the rest of the quest.
- **See (Line of Sight)** ★ — target (another hero or a monster) must be **visible**:
  an **unobstructed straight line** from caster to target.
  - **Rule of thumb**: draw a line from the **center of the caster's square to the
    center of the target's square**. If the line does **NOT cross a wall, closed door,
    hero, or monster**, the target is visible — **even if the line just touches a corner
    or wall edge** (grazing a corner/edge does NOT block). [= lenient corner rule =
    `hasLineOfSight`.] Self-casts need no LOS.

## Page 15 — Action 3 Search for Treasure (+ Wandering Monsters / Hazards)  ★
- **Action 3: Search for Treasure**: treasure is found **only in rooms, not corridors**.
  A room may be searched by all four heroes, but **each hero may search a given room
  only ONCE**, on their own turn. Some treasures are trap-protected (see p16).
- Procedure: room must be **uninhabited by monsters**; verbally declare "I am searching
  for treasure"; searching covers the **whole room** regardless of square (**do not move
  your figure**). If no special treasure in the quest notes, **draw a random treasure
  card** and read aloud (gold, potions, etc.); record gold/potions on the sheet.
- **Gold may be shared** among heroes; spent between quests at the armory.
- **Treasure deck**: gold & potion cards are kept (NOT returned until next quest).
  **Almost half of the treasure cards are wandering monsters / hazards** — these ARE
  returned to the deck and reshuffled (reusable; shuffle before each draw).
- **Special treasure** (named in quest notes): read aloud once; discovered **only by the
  first hero** to search that room (even if others search it later).
- **Wandering Monster** (treasure card): Zargon places the monster (named in quest
  notes) **next to the searcher** and immediately rolls its Attack dice — this round it
  **can only attack the searcher**; afterwards it stays and moves/attacks like other
  monsters. (If no adjacent square is free, place it as close as possible.) The hero
  rolls **defend dice** vs the attack, then continues their turn.
- **Hazard** (treasure card): read aloud and follow the card's directions.

## Page 16 — Treasures (artifacts/potions), Action 4 Secret Doors, Action 5 Traps intro
- **Treasure types**: gold coins, magic spells, **artifacts**, **potions**.
  - **Artifacts** — special treasures detailed on artifact cards (weapons, armor,
    power items); may be a quest objective; a hero may **give artifacts to other
    heroes** on their turn.
  - **Potions** — drink at any time (free); effects per the card / Quest Book; may drink
    **more than one at a time**. ★ **Healing clutch**: if Body Points reach 0, you may
    drink a **healing potion before you die** to restore 1+ BP and survive. May give a
    potion to a fellow hero, but only on your turn.
- **Action 4: Search for Secret Doors** — only if **no monsters visible** to you.
  Declare "I am searching for secret doors." Zargon reveals secret doors in the **room
  OR corridor you are in** (places a secret-door tile); do NOT move your figure. The
  door opens only when you move adjacent and declare opening it (reveals items beyond;
  cannot be re-closed). Secret doors are invisible to looking — **only found by
  searching**.
- **Action 5: Search for Traps** — only if **no monsters visible**. Declare "I am
  searching for traps." Zargon tells you which squares have traps but **places no tiles**
  (still concealed & unsprung).
  - Traps exist in **both rooms and corridors**. Stepping onto a pit / falling-block /
    spear square **auto-springs** it (possible body damage).
  - **Chest/furniture traps**: if you **search for treasure without first searching for
    traps, you spring the trap** → search traps first.

## Page 17 — Traps in depth (pits) + What Happens to a Hero in a Pit
- **Four kinds of traps**: **pit, falling block, spear, chest/furniture** (locations
  marked in **gold** on the quest maps; only Zargon knows them).
- **Monsters do NOT spring hidden traps** (heroes only).
- **Searching for traps from a doorway doesn't work**: you cannot search a room's traps
  by looking through the door — you must be **inside the room**. The first hero to enter
  a room may hit a trap placed just inside the door and take damage.
- **Pit trap**: a covered hole.
  - Found by searching → Zargon points to the square, **no tile placed** (unsprung);
    may be **jumped** or **disarmed** next turn (p19).
  - Moved onto undiscovered → **auto-spring**: Zargon places the pit tile under the
    figure, **1 Body Point damage, the hero's turn ends**; record damage.
  - **In a pit**: may search it for treasure/secret doors as a separate room; may attack
    & defend but **roll one fewer combat die** (applies to monsters in pits too);
    **minimum 1 die** even if the penalty would reduce to 0; move out on your next turn.
  - A **sprung** pit (tile placed) **cannot be disarmed/removed** — only **jumped over**.

## Page 18 — Falling-block / Spear / Chest-furniture traps (detail)  ★
- **Falling-Block Trap**: collapsing ceiling. Found by search → Zargon points, no tile
  (unsprung; can jump or disarm next turn). Stepping onto unsearched → spring:
  - Zargon gives you **3 combat dice**; **each skull = 1 Body Point of damage**; you may
    **NOT roll Defend dice**.
  - Then move ahead OR back to an empty space; the trap square is now a **permanent
    block** (impassable forever — you can become trapped or cut off).
  - Once sprung & placed, **cannot be disarmed or jumped**.
- **Spear Trap**: carefully hidden. Found by search → Zargon says the square looks
  suspicious & points (no tile). Stepping on → auto-spring: **roll 1 combat die**, turn
  ends; **black/white shield = dodged** (no damage, may continue, trap **gone
  forever**); skull = take damage. **There are no spear-trap tiles** (square is safe
  afterward).
- **Chest/Furniture Trap**: variety — poisonous gas, poison needle, explosive latch,
  shooting dart, etc. Discovered by searching for traps in the room/corridor where the
  chest/furniture is (Zargon points to it). May **disarm on next turn**. Springs if you
  search the chest/furniture for treasure before disarming; effect per quest notes.

## Page 19 — Jumping a Trap + Action 6 Disarm a Trap  ★
- **Jumping a Trap**: if a trap blocks your path you may jump it.
  - Need **≥2 squares of movement remaining** (one onto the trap, one onto an
    **unoccupied** square beyond). A pit may have up to **3** landing squares; a corner
    pit only **1**.
  - Roll **1 combat die**: **anything but a skull (a shield) = success** — you clear the
    trap, **expending 2 movement squares**, and may continue if movement remains. **Skull
    = spring** the trap (take that trap's damage, tile placed if any, you end on the trap
    square, turn ends).
  - A sprung **pit CAN be jumped**; a sprung **falling-block CANNOT** be jumped.
  - If the landing square is occupied by a monster and the hero lacks special
    weapons/armor, the hero must voluntarily fall into the pit and fight from inside (at
    a disadvantage). Monsters with enough movement + a vacant landing always clear pits
    and take no damage entering voluntarily.
- **Action 6: Disarm a Trap**: requires **knowing the trap's location** AND possessing a
  **tool kit** (bought at the armory) — **except the Dwarf**, who can disarm without a
  kit.
  - **With a tool kit (non-dwarf)**: before moving, announce you're moving onto the trap
    square to disarm; move onto it; roll **1 combat die** — **skull = springs** the trap
    (suffer damage); **black or white shield = disarmed** (trap removed, never placed on
    the board). [Dwarf: same attempt, no tool kit required.]

## Page 20 — Zargon's Turn: monster movement & attack  ★core
- **Zargon's turn** comes after all four heroes finish. Zargon may move **every monster**
  on the board. Like heroes, each monster does **move-then-act OR act-then-move** and
  **cannot split** movement around its action.
- **Monsters do NOT roll for movement** — each has a fixed **maximum movement** listed in
  the monster chart (GM screen) and on the 8 monster cards; need not move the full
  distance. (Matches our MONSTER_STATS `move` values: goblin 10, orc 8, etc.)
- **Two monster actions**: **Attack** or **Cast a Dread spell**.
- **Monsters may NOT**: search treasure / secret doors; move or attack **diagonally**;
  **pass over heroes**; move through walls; **open or close doors**; share a square. They
  don't spring/search/disarm traps.
- **Action 1: Attack (monster)** — attack any **adjacent** hero (orthogonal), **once per
  turn**; attack strength is **innate** (no weapon).
- **How a Monster Attacks**: roll the monster's **Attack dice** (from chart); **no skulls
  = failed attack**. The hero then defends (white shields).

## Page 21 — How a Hero Defends, Dead Heroes, Dread spells  ★core
- **How a Hero Defends**: a hero usually rolls **2 Defend dice** (fewer in a pit / under
  certain spells; more after buying armor). Each **WHITE shield = blocks 1 hit**.
- **Damage**: each attacker skull = 1 hit = 1 BP; subtract defender's shields. BP can't
  go below 0 (overkill wasted). Hero at 0 BP = dead (unless saved).
- **Cast a Dread Spell** (Zargon, instead of a monster attacking): Dread spells are given
  to **specific monsters** per the quest notes; a monster may only cast on a hero **it
  can see** (LOS); once per quest, then discarded. Several Dread spells are unused in the
  first 14 quests (future / custom quests).
- **What Happens to a Dead Hero**: out for the rest of that quest; may **rename & replay
  as a new character** next quest. Possessions are picked up by another hero **in the
  room/corridor**; if you die alone, a monster there claims them (removed from game,
  can't be used).
- **How a Hero Escapes Death** (two ways at 0 BP): (1) immediately **drink a
  life-restoring potion** (e.g., healing potion) — any time; (2) if a spellcaster with a
  **healing spell who has NOT yet acted** this turn, cast it on yourself.

## Coverage note
This PDF excerpt contains **booklet pages 2–21** (10 sheets, top page = odd 2N+1, bottom
page = even 2N). **NOT included**: page 1 (front cover) and **page 22** ("What happens if
you run out of monsters?" + **Ending the Quest** — what happens between quests, lost
artifacts, unfinished quests), plus the **Game Master's screen monster chart**, the
**Quest Book** (14 quests + maps + notes), and the card faces (treasure/equipment/
artifact/spell/Dread-spell/monster cards). These are needed for the campaign/between-quest
and per-quest layers and should be sourced separately.
