# HeroScape — Scenarios

> Mechanics below are summarized in our own words for the digital adaptation — this is a rules reference, not a reproduction of the rulebook.

The Master Set rulebook ships **five battlefields, each with exactly two printed scenarios** (confirming the "two scenarios per battlefield" note on p. 3): three battlefields carry one Basic + one Master scenario, two carry two Master scenarios. The three Basic scenarios are the "3 Basic Game Scenarios" referenced on p. 4.

- ⚠ Exact hex-by-hex map data (terrain build, glyph hexes, numbered start hexes, start-zone shading) is **not recoverable from the scans** of pages 18, 20, 22, 24, 26 — the build diagrams and map graphics are below readable resolution. Authoritative map data must come from a higher-resolution scan or community map files. Everything *rules-shaped* on the scenario pages (19, 21, 23, 25, 27, 28) is legible and captured below.

- **Basic scenarios have no rounds and no round limit.** They use the Basic Game loop (p. 4): each player rolls **6 combat dice**, most skulls takes the first turn (re-roll ties), then players strictly alternate single turns until a victory condition fires. Basic-side cards only — no Life, no special powers, all-or-nothing destruction.
- **Master scenarios run in rounds** (3 turns per player per round; order markers + d20 initiative each round, p. 9). Each scenario page prints a Round Marker Track sized to its round limit (12 / 12 / 12 / 8 / 6 / 12).

See 04-combat-range-los-attack.md.
- Master armies are always **player choice: draft or bring a pre-made army**, total card points ≤ the scenario budget (p. 8).

## Table of the Giants

*Giants once gathered here in the deep of winter to divide the hunting lands between them; their bones are rumored to lie buried beneath the stones.*

### 1. Attack at Dawn — Basic, 2 players (p. 19)

*A raid launched at first light in early winter.*

- **Goal / Victory:** Destroy all of the opponent's figures. The instant a player has no figures left on the battlefield, the other player wins. No other condition; no round limit.
- **Setup:** No glyphs. Preset armies placed on the numbered start hexes printed on the map (Player 1 south, Player 2 north). Per the Basic Game rule (p. 4), Player 1 takes the Good army, Player 2 the Evil army.

| Player | Map markers | Figures |
|---|---|---|
| Player 1 | 1 | Raelin |
| Player 1 | 2 | Finn the Viking Champion |
| Player 1 | 3 | Thorgrim the Viking Champion |
| Player 1 | 4–7 | Tarn Viking Warriors (4 figures) |
| Player 2 | 1 | Ne-Gok-Sa |
| Player 2 | 2 | Grimnak |
| Player 2 | 3–5 | Izumi Samurai (3 figures) |

**Notes**

- Deliberately asymmetric figure counts (7 vs 5) balanced by Basic-side stats; placement hexes are fixed, not player choice.
- Pure last-army-standing — the simplest possible victory check for the engine: evaluate immediately whenever a figure is removed.

### 2. Clashing Fronts — Master, 2 players or 4 players in 2 teams (p. 19)

*Two Valkyrie Generals' armies, each on the march, collide where neither expected battle.*

- **Goal:** Destroy all opposing figures.
- **Setup:** Glyphs placed **power-side up** on the marked map hexes (powers live from the start — see the Glyphs topic file for each power, p. 15). One player/team deploys in the brown starting zone, the other in the navy zone.

| Player count | Army budget per player |
|---|---|
| 2 players | 400 points |
| 4 players (2 teams of 2) | 300 points |

- **Victory:** Wiping out all enemy figures wins immediately. If the game instead reaches the **end of Round 12**, it stops and timeout scoring decides the winner (Scoring, p. 14 — see 04-combat-range-los-attack.md).

**Notes**

- This is the rulebook's "default" Master scenario — it is also the worked example for drafting on p. 8 (2-player, 400 points, Table of the Giants).
- Teammates share one starting zone and may talk strategy openly, but each takes their own separate turns (pp. 7–8).
- ⚠ Which glyphs sit on which hexes requires the map graphic (see index notes).

## The Forsaken Waters

*The Dark Lakes between Laur and Nostralund, disputed for a rumored wellspring — both shores were fortified, then left in ruins by endless skirmishing.*

### 3. Dive the Dark Lakes — Basic, 2 players (p. 21)

*A wellspring is said to lie in the depths, and each General holds a device capable of the deep dive.*

- **Goal:** Capture the enemy's deep-dive device.
- **Setup:** The **2 Glyphs of Brandar** stand in for the two deep-dive devices — one per player — placed on the marked hexes **power (Artifact) side up**. Preset armies on numbered start hexes:

| Player | Map markers | Figures |
|---|---|---|
| Player 1 (Laur) | 1 | Sgt. Drake Alexander |
| Player 1 (Laur) | 2 | Krav Maga Agents |
| Player 1 (Laur) | 3 | Airborne Elite |
| Player 1 (Laur) | 4 | Syvarris |
| Player 2 (Nostralund) | 1 | Mimring |
| Player 2 (Nostralund) | 2–4 | Marro Warriors |
| Player 2 (Nostralund) | 5–6 | Zettian Guards |

- **Victory (first reached wins, both checked immediately):**
  1. One of **your** figures moves onto the **other player's** device glyph; or
  2. The other player has no figures left on the battlefield.

**Notes**

- Each device "belongs" to a side: stepping on **your own** Brandar glyph does nothing. The engine needs per-glyph ownership for the win check.
- Brandar is the no-power Artifact glyph (p. 15) — landing on it forces the usual movement stop but grants nothing; here it is purely a win-condition space.
- The win triggers **mid-turn, on entry** — not at end of round.
- ⚠ Squad placement markers each cover several hexes in the map diagram; per-figure start hexes need the map data.

### 4. Winter Holdout — Master, 2 players or 4 players in 2 teams (p. 21)

*Warm waters keep this pass free of snow; both sides dig in and wait for reinforcements.*

- **Goal:** Destroy all opposing figures.
- **Setup:** Glyphs placed **power-side up** where shown. Brown vs navy starting zones.

| Player count | Army budget per player |
|---|---|
| 2 players | 300 points |
| 4 players (2 teams of 2) | 160 points ⚠ |

  - ⚠ The 4-player budget reads as **160** but the digit is small in the scan; the matching 160-point reinforcement size below supports the reading (medium confidence).

- **Special rule — Reinforcements (end-of-round d20 roll):**

| Field | Rule |
|---|---|
| When | At the **end of every round** |
| Who may roll | Every player who still has **≥ 1 figure on the battlefield** |
| Optional? | **Yes — player choice** to roll at all |
| Success after Rounds 1–2 | d20 roll of **16+** |
| Success after Round 3 onward | d20 roll of **11+** |
| What arrives | A **160-point** force (drafted — roll for draft order if needed — or pre-made), using only Army Cards **not previously destroyed this game** |
| Banned cards | Any Army Card with the **"The Drop"** special power |
| Where placed | The rolling player's **original starting zone**; joins their army |
| Frequency cap | **Once per player per game**, maximum (possibly never) |

- **Victory:** Wipeout wins immediately. At the **end of Round 12** the game stops and timeout scoring applies (Scoring, p. 14 — see 04-combat-range-los-attack.md).

**Notes**

- A player with zero figures on the battlefield can never roll for reinforcements — being wiped out is final (and ends a 2-player game anyway; in the 4-player team game a wiped player's teammate may fight on).
- "Not previously destroyed" screens at the **Army Card** level — cards whose figures all died are ineligible to return.
- ⚠ Ordering of end-of-round events (reinforcement roll vs. round-marker advance vs. the Round-12 stop) is not specified; a reinforcement roll at the end of Round 12 is moot since the game ends, but the engine should pick and document an order. See "Engine patterns" below.

## Durgeth Swamps

*A savage swamp tribe that lived too near a wellspring was wiped out early in the war — yet travelers whisper its survivors still prey on those who cross the bogs.*

### 5. The Search for Comfrey Plants — Basic, 2 players (p. 23)

*Both sides race to gather the swamp's healing comfrey plants for their wounded.*

- **Goal:** Be the first to find 2 comfrey plants.
- **Setup (hidden-objective shuffle):**
  - The **2 Glyphs of Brandar + the Glyph of Kelda** represent the 3 hidden comfrey plants.
  - **All 10 glyphs in the set** are turned **symbol-side up** (identity hidden — every glyph looks identical this way), **shuffled**, and placed on the **10 map hexes marked "?"** (3 in the western part of the map, 7 in the eastern part). So 3 of the 10 are plants and **7 are decoys** (Astrid, Gerda, Ivor, Valda, Dagmar, Erland, Mitonsoul).
  - Placement is an impersonal joint setup step — no player is named as the placer; the shuffle is the randomizer.
  - Preset armies on numbered start hexes:

| Player | Figures (placement order 1–3) |
|---|---|
| Player 1 | Thorgrim the Viking Champion; Sgt. Drake Alexander; Syvarris |
| Player 2 | Ne-Gok-Sa; Deathwalker 9000; Agent Carr |

- **Special rule — reveal on landing (automatic):** when one of your figures lands on a glyph it **must stop** there, and the glyph is flipped:
  - **Not a plant** (any glyph other than Brandar/Kelda): remove it from the battlefield. **Its power never takes effect** — decoys are inert.
  - **A plant** (Brandar or Kelda): the moving player keeps it power-side up in front of them (collected; off the board).
- **Victory (first reached wins):** collect **2 of the 3** plants; or the opponent has no figures left on the battlefield.

**Notes**

- Canonical hidden-token pattern: the engine should shuffle 10 tokens (3 plant / 7 decoy) onto 10 fixed hexes; identities are hidden from **both** players until flipped; collected plants are public information.
- Because this is a Basic scenario, glyphs are scenario **objects**, not power tiles (p. 17) — the decoy removal rule makes that explicit, and Kelda's normal "wounded figures only may stop here" restriction does not apply.
- Flips are deterministic on entry (forced stop + flip), so a turn can end early mid-move; collection is automatic, not a choice.

### 6. Under Tempest's Cover — Master, 2 players, asymmetric hidden-identity escort (p. 23)

*A storm masks an enemy scout carrying battlefield intelligence toward a transfer post.*

- **Player 1's goal:** get a secret Hero Scout onto the Valkyrie's transfer post **by the end of Round 12**. **Player 2's goal:** prevent that.
- **Setup:**
  - **Glyph of Gerda** placed **power-side up** where shown (its normal Defense +1 power is live — see the Glyphs topic file, p. 15).
  - **One Glyph of Brandar** placed **power-side up** where shown — it marks the **transfer post** (destination hex; no power).
  - Armies: each player drafts or brings a pre-made **400-point** army.
  - **Secret scout assignment (Player 1, hidden choice):** Player 1 takes **one glyph per Unique Hero Army Card he controls** — **one of which must be the Glyph of Kelda** — and secretly places one glyph **symbol-side up on each** of those cards. The Unique Hero whose card carries Kelda is the secret Hero Scout. (Symbol-side-up glyphs are indistinguishable, so the markers leak nothing.)
  - Starting zones: Player 1 = brown, Player 2 = navy.
- **Special rules:**
  - **Storm:** **no flying and no parachuting** this scenario. Army Cards with **"The Drop"** ignore that power and deploy as normal figures.
  - **Round 1 initiative bias:** Player 2 adds **+12** to his Round-1 initiative roll only. (Per p. 9, initiative modifiers also apply to re-rolls.)
  - **Forced reveal (automatic):** whenever a Player-1 Unique Hero is **destroyed** — or **control of it is taken by Player 2** (e.g., a mind-control power) — flip the glyph on that hero's card, revealing whether it was the scout.
- **Victory:**
  - **Player 1 wins immediately** when he moves a Unique Hero onto the transfer post **and** that hero is the scout (its card glyph is revealed as Kelda).
  - **Player 2 wins** if the scout is destroyed.
  - **Player 2 wins at the end of Round 12** if the scout has not reached the post — **even if Player 2 has no figures left on the battlefield** (the timeout win requires no survival).

**Notes**

- Player 1's army composition is constrained implicitly: it must include **at least one Unique Hero** (the scout). The rulebook states no minimum beyond that.
- ⚠ Not stated: what happens when a **non-scout** Unique Hero steps onto the post — no reveal is prescribed, so the safest reading is *nothing happens* (the bluff stands; Player 1 simply doesn't win). Engine should not auto-flip.
- ⚠ Not stated: which physical glyphs Player 1 uses for the non-Kelda card markers (identity is irrelevant face-down; any unused glyphs work).
- Player 1 losing **all figures** is not listed as a Player 2 win by itself — but it is equivalent in effect: with the scout destroyed (or no figures to move) Player 2's conditions are met. The scout-destroyed check is the one to implement.
- Asymmetric timeout: this is a "defender wins on timeout" scenario — see "Engine patterns".

## Trollsford Swamps

*Trolls once raided across this river, until Archkyrie Nevass raised a stronghold to hold them back.*

### 7. A Desperate Rescue Attempt — Master, 3 players (2 vs 1) (p. 25)

*A time-limited enchantment will teleport Drake's group to safety the instant an ally touches him — the in-fiction basis for the touch trigger and the round limit.*

- **Teams:** Players 1 & 2 are a team (the rescuers); Player 3 plays alone (the captor).
- **Goals:** Team — rescue Sgt. Drake Alexander **by the end of Round 8**. Player 3 — prevent the rescue.
- **Setup:**

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Power glyphs | 4 glyphs placed **power-side up** where shown (powers live) ⚠ identities need map data |
| Prison marker | 1 extra glyph placed **symbol-side up** where shown, with the **Sgt. Drake Alexander figure standing on it** |
| Player 1 army | 200 points (draft or pre-made) |
| Player 2 army | 200 points (draft or pre-made) |
| Player 3 army | 500 points (draft or pre-made) |
| Starting zones | Players 1 & 2 share the **navy** zone; Player 3 = **brown** zone |

- **Special rules — Drake is scenery:** Drake cannot participate in the battle, cannot be controlled by **any** player, and cannot move from his space. The glyph beneath him cannot be moved and its power has **no effect** on the game.
- **Victory:**
  - The team wins **immediately** when **either** teammate moves a figure **adjacent to** Drake.
  - Player 3 wins if Drake is not rescued by the **end of Round 8** — **even if Player 3 has no figures left on the battlefield**.
  - Player 3 also wins **immediately** if Players 1 and 2 have no figures left on the battlefield.

**Notes**

- 400 total team points vs 500 defender points — asymmetry is intentional (defender also defends a fixed point).
- The rescue triggers **mid-turn on movement**, not at end of round; reaching adjacency is enough (no action, no roll, no need to survive afterward).
- ⚠ "Adjacent" is not qualified — presumably plain adjacency; whether the engagement-adjacency *exceptions* (elevation ≥ Height, intervening ruins, p. 12) could block a "rescue adjacency" is unstated. Recommend implementing as simple hex adjacency and flagging for playtest. See 04-combat-range-los-attack.md for the adjacency exceptions.
- ⚠ Drake's interaction with normal figure rules is unstated beyond the three prohibitions: assume he **occupies his space** (cannot be moved through or landed on), cannot be attacked or targeted, never engages, and blocks LOS like any physical figure. Marked as an engine assumption, not rulebook text.
- The inert symbol-side-up glyph under Drake never flips (figures can't land on his occupied space).

### 8. A Toxic Mist — Master, 2 players, rising-hazard attrition (p. 25)

*A Valkyrie General has poisoned the water, and the gas it gives off slowly fills the valley.*

- **Goal:** Destroy all of your opponent's figures.
- **Setup:** Glyphs **power-side up** where shown ⚠ (identities need map data). Each player drafts or brings a pre-made **400-point** army. Brown vs navy starting zones.
- **Special rule — the rising gas (fully automatic, end-of-round):**

| End of round | Gas covers |
|---|---|
| 1 | all **water spaces** + all **level-1** spaces |
| 2 | …plus all level-2 spaces |
| n (3–5) | …plus all level-n spaces |
| 5 and beyond | capped — the gas **never rises above level 5** |

  - At the **end of each round**, every figure standing on a gas-covered space takes **1 wound** (place 1 Wound Marker; no roll, no defense, affects both players' figures equally).
  - The gas has **no effect during a round** — figures move through and stand on gassed spaces freely mid-round; only end-of-round positions matter.
- **Victory:**
  - You win when the other player has no figures left on the battlefield.
  - **Simultaneous mutual wipeout** (e.g., both players' last figures die to the same end-of-round gas tick): **neither player wins** — an explicit draw.
  - Hard time limit: at the **end of Round 8** the game stops and timeout scoring applies (Scoring, p. 14 — see 04-combat-range-los-attack.md).

**Notes**

- "Level n" refers to the elevation of the space a figure's base occupies. Figures on level-6+ spaces are permanently safe from gas (cap at 5); water is gassed from round 1.
- The 1-wound tick uses normal wound/Life bookkeeping — multi-Life heroes survive several ticks; Life-1 squad figures standing in gas at round end are destroyed outright.
- The engine **must support a no-winner terminal state** (draw) — unique among the rulebook's scenarios.
- ⚠ End-of-round ordering at Round 8 is unstated: if the gas tick resolves before the timeout scoring, gas kills at the buzzer change the score. The gas rule says wounds happen "at the end of each round" and the scoring happens when "the game reaches the end of Round 8" — recommend: gas tick first, then score; flag for playtest.

## Migol's Tomb

*The tomb of Migol III on Barrenspur, long since stripped by thieves — though its catacombs are rumored to hold riches still.*

### 9. Mimring's Fortress — Master, 4 players as individuals (1 defender vs 3 rivals) (p. 27)

*A device is slowly burying a map of three enemy wellsprings deep in the rock; the dragon need only guard it until the work is done — the basis for "protect until the end of Round 6".*

- **Goals:** Player 1 (the dragon's side) must protect "the map" until the end of Round 6. Players 2, 3, and 4 **each independently** try to seize the map — they are rivals, **not a team**.
- **Setup:**

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| The map (objective) | A **Glyph of Brandar** placed **power-side up** on the marked hex |
| Hidden catacomb glyphs | **Astrid, Gerda, Valda, Mitonsoul** turned **symbol-side up, shuffled**, placed on the 4 "?" hexes inside the catacombs (random hidden identities) |
| Mimring | Placed on the marked map hex |
| Player 1 army | **Mimring plus 260 points** of drafted or pre-made armies |
| Players 2 / 3 / 4 armies | **200 points each** |
| Starting zones | Player 1 = brown; Players 2/3/4 each take one of **navy, teal, dark yellow** |

- **Special rule — the uneasy alliance (one-way breakable truce):** Players 2, 3, and 4 start the game **allied: their armies all count as friendly to one another**. The moment **any one of them attacks another**, the truce is **off between all three, permanently** — it cannot be re-formed. (Player-choice trigger; global, irreversible effect.)
- **Victory (checked at end of round):**
  - If Player 2, 3, or 4 has a figure **standing on the map's Brandar glyph at the end of any round**, that player takes the map and **wins individually**.
  - If nobody has taken the map by the **end of Round 6**, **Player 1 wins — even if Player 1 has no figures left on the battlefield**.

**Notes**

- The objective check is an **end-of-round snapshot**, not touch-to-win: stepping on the glyph mid-round and leaving (or being killed) before the round ends scores nothing. Contrast with Dive the Dark Lakes' instant capture.
- Landing on the power-side-up Brandar still forces the normal movement stop (p. 15) but grants no power.
- The shuffled catacomb glyphs follow **normal glyph rules** when stepped on (forced stop, flip, power takes effect immediately — see the Glyphs topic file, p. 15). One of the four is **Mitonsoul (Massive Curse)** — a hidden trap that forces a d20 roll **for every figure on the battlefield** (1 = destroyed) the moment someone flips it.
- "Friendly" while the truce holds means, at minimum: allies cannot be attacked, allied figures do not engage each other, and figures may **move through** allied figures' spaces (the p. 9 friendly pass-through rule, barring engagement). ⚠ The rulebook does not enumerate this; the engine should treat truce-allies exactly like teammates for movement/engagement purposes until the truce breaks, and flag for playtest.
- ⚠ Not stated which of P2/P3/P4 picks their zone first (navy/teal/dark yellow) — recommend the placement-order d20 roll from p. 8.
- ⚠ "Mimring plus 260 points": Mimring's own card cost is **excluded** from the 260 by the wording; total defender value depends on Mimring's printed Points (on his Army Card, not in these scans).
- Whether an attack **declaration alone** breaks the truce vs. a resolved attack is not split out — the printed trigger is "attacks another"; recommend breaking the truce at attack declaration.

### 10. To Take Barrenspur — Master, 2 players or 4 players in 2 teams (p. 28)

*Rival Valkyrie Generals have each been ordered to take Barrenspur and hold Migol's Tomb.*

- **Goal:** Destroy all of your opponents' figures.
- **Setup:** Glyphs **power-side up** where shown ⚠ (identities need map data). Brown vs navy starting zones.

| Player count | Army budget per player |
|---|---|
| 2 players | 400 points |
| 4 players (2 teams of 2) | 300 points |

- **Special rule — mud (automatic terrain-cost override):** rains have turned all **sand** spaces to mud — every figure pays **2 movement points per sand space entered** (instead of the normal 1). Applies to all players' figures, all game.
- **Victory:** Your side wins when the opposing side has no figures left. At the **end of Round 12** the game stops and timeout scoring applies (Scoring, p. 14 — see 04-combat-range-los-attack.md).

**Notes**

- The override is on the **space-entry cost** only. ⚠ Interaction with climbing is unstated; the consistent reading is additive: entering a sand space one level up costs 2 (mud) + 1 (level side) = 3. Flag for playtest.
- ⚠ Whether mud also affects figures that ignore elevation costs (flying) is unstated — flying "counts spaces" (p. 16), and mud changes a space's entry cost, so flyers landing on/normally crossing sand presumably pay it only if they use ground movement. Recommend: mud applies to ground movement only.
- Engine-wise this is Clashing Fronts with a terrain-cost modifier — good candidate for a shared "kill-'em-all + timeout scoring" scenario template.

## Engine-relevant scenario patterns

Cross-cutting mechanics the scenario system must support (each cited to the scenario that proves it):

1. **Glyphs as hidden/objective tokens.** Scenarios repurpose glyphs three ways: shuffled face-down (symbol-side up) pools with secret identities on "?" hexes (Comfrey Plants — 3 real among 10; Mimring's Fortress — 4 shuffled powers); face-up Brandar Artifacts as pure objective markers with per-side ownership or win semantics (Dive the Dark Lakes, Under Tempest's Cover, Mimring's Fortress); and a glyph as a **hidden-role card marker** placed on an Army Card, off the board entirely (Under Tempest's Cover). Symbol-side-up glyphs are visually identical, which is what makes the hidden-info patterns sound.
2. **Instant vs end-of-round victory checks.** Some objectives fire the moment a figure arrives (Dive the Dark Lakes device capture; Desperate Rescue adjacency; Under Tempest's Cover scout-on-post; all wipeouts), but Mimring's Fortress checks possession **only at the end of each round**. The engine needs both trigger timings per scenario.
3. **Timeout wins (defender wins at the round limit).** Asymmetric scenarios give the defending side an unconditional win when the track runs out — **explicitly even if that side has zero figures left** (Under Tempest's Cover R12; Desperate Rescue R8; Mimring's Fortress R6). Symmetric scenarios instead fall back to **timeout scoring** (most points of surviving cards, full card value per card with ≥1 survivor — Scoring, p. 14): Clashing Fronts, Winter Holdout, To Take Barrenspur (R12), Toxic Mist (R8). See 04-combat-range-los-attack.md.
4. **Simultaneous-wipeout draw.** A Toxic Mist explicitly makes mutual annihilation a **no-winner** result; the engine's terminal states must include a draw, at least for that scenario.
5. **Reinforcement d20 rolls.** Winter Holdout: optional end-of-round d20 per surviving player; threshold **16+ after rounds 1–2, 11+ afterward**; success deploys a one-time **160-point** force of never-destroyed, non-"The Drop" cards into the original start zone; **once per player per game**.
6. **Breakable-truce state.** Mimring's Fortress: a scenario-scoped boolean ("truce intact") that starts true, makes three players mutually friendly, and flips false **permanently and globally** the first time any of them attacks another. Player-choice trigger, irreversible, affects movement pass-through, engagement, and attack legality.
7. **Terrain-cost overrides.** To Take Barrenspur doubles the entry cost of all sand spaces (mud). Scenario config should be able to override per-terrain movement cost without touching core movement code.
8. **Rising-hazard wounds.** A Toxic Mist: an automatic end-of-round area effect that grows each round (water + levels 1..n, capped at level 5) and deals 1 unblockable wound to every figure standing in it — no roll, no defense, no mid-round effect.
9. **Per-scenario rule disablement and modifiers.** Under Tempest's Cover bans flying and parachuting outright and nullifies "The Drop" (cards deploy normally), and grants a one-round flat **+12 initiative** bonus (which also applies to initiative re-rolls, p. 9). Scenario config must be able to switch off named global mechanics and card powers and to bias initiative.
10. **Forced information reveals.** Under Tempest's Cover flips a hidden card marker on a trigger (Unique Hero destroyed **or control taken by the opponent**) — hidden-info lifecycles need scripted reveal triggers, including control-change events.
11. **Asymmetric budgets, preset armies, and >2-player topologies.** Budgets range 160–500 points and differ across seats (200+200 vs 500; Mimring+260 vs 200×3); Basic scenarios use fixed printed armies and fixed start hexes; player topologies include 1v1, 2v2 teams, 2v1 team-vs-one, and 4-way individuals with a truce. Start zones are per-seat colored regions (brown/navy/teal/dark yellow), shared by teammates (p. 8).
