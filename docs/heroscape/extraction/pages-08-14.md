## Page 8 — Gather & Place Your Army; setup components; round structure intro

### Step 2 of setup: Gather and Place Your Army
- Two PLAYER-CHOICE methods of building an Army for Master Game scenarios: use a **pre-made Army** or **draft** one.
- Hard constraint: each player's total Army point value must be **≤ the point limit in the chosen Game Scenario** (lower is legal, higher is not).
- Players do NOT need equal numbers of Army Cards (e.g., one player may field 3 cards vs. an opponent's 4 or 5).
- **Army Card colors:** in the Master Game, good/evil loyalties are loosened — a player's Army may mix card colors freely.

### Using a Pre-Made Army
- Players who arrive with a chosen Army place their cards face-up in front of them.
- Placement order: all players roll (the 20-sided die); ties re-roll. **Highest roller places their ENTIRE Army first**, then placement passes left, each player placing their whole Army in turn.

### Drafting an Army
- Draft purpose: balanced/fair selection. When a player picks an Army Card, they take its figure(s) and **place them on the battlefield in a starting zone IMMEDIATELY**, before the next player picks.
- Placement constraints (apply to both methods):
  - Your entire Army must go in **one single starting zone**.
  - You may NOT place figures in a starting zone occupied by an enemy.
  - Teammates share the same starting zone unless the Game Scenario says otherwise.
  - If you have more figures than starting-zone spaces, the **excess figures are simply not used**.

**2-player draft procedure** (worked example referenced: 2-player "Clashing Fronts" scenario on the "Table of the Giants" battlefield, 400-point Armies each):
1. Both players roll the 20-sided die (re-roll ties). Higher roller drafts first: chooses **1** Army Card.
2. The other player then chooses **2** Army Cards.
3. Players then alternate choosing **1** card at a time until both Armies are complete.

**3+ player draft procedure** (snake draft):
1. All players roll the 20-sided die (re-roll ties); highest roller drafts first, choosing **1** Army Card.
2. Draft passes left, each player choosing 1 card; the **last player in the chain chooses 2** Army Cards.
3. The draft then reverses direction (snake); the player at each end of the chain picks 2 cards when the direction turns around.
4. If picking any additional Army Card would push a player over the scenario's point limit, that player must **pass** — passing means their Army is complete.
5. Drafting continues snaking back and forth until every player has completed their Army.

⚠ UNCLEAR: the fine print of the 3+ player snake-draft steps (exactly which positions take double picks on each reversal) is small in the scan; the structure above (double pick at each end of the snake, mandatory pass when over budget) is the confident reading.

### Example 2 (diagram, photos)
Shows both players' placed armies on a terrain board:
- "Your Army": 400-point total, with **Grimnak** (described as the most powerful figure) placed at the front of the starting zone.
- "Your Opponent's Army": **390-point** total, with **Syvarris and Deathwalker** (long-Range attackers) placed at the forefront.
Illustrates: armies may total less than the limit, and placement within the starting zone is a player choice with tactical meaning.

### Components placed at setup
| Component | Count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Order Markers per player | 4 | Numbered **1, 2, 3**, plus an **"X"** marker; placed on Army Cards each round to schedule turns |
| Combat dice | 12 | Shared pool near the battlefield; the SAME dice serve as attack dice (count skulls) and defense dice (count shields) |
| Wound Markers | all | Kept in a supply near the battlefield |
| Glyphs | per scenario | "Magical" tiles placed on specific spaces as directed by the Battlefield & Game Scenario section; most grant a special power to a figure that lands on them (details on page 15) |
| Round Marker | 1 (black) | Placed on space "1" of the scenario's Round Marker Track |

### The Battle Begins / A Round of Play
- The game is played in **rounds**; a round = **3 turns per player**.
- **Round Marker Track:** each Game Scenario in the Battlefield & Game Scenario section has a numbered track. Before round 1, place the black Round Marker on "1"; at the end of each round, advance it one space (automatic, not a choice).

## Page 9 — Round sequence; Order Markers; initiative; turn structure; movement basics

### The four steps of every round (in order)
1. Place Your Order Markers
2. Roll for Initiative
3. Players take turns one at a time until **each player has taken 3 turns**
4. Advance the Round Marker to the next number

### Step 1 — Place Your Order Markers
- Each turn uses the figures of exactly ONE Army Card. Each round, the player secretly pre-commits which card acts on which turn by placing Order Markers **1, 2, 3** on their Army Cards (1 = first turn, 2 = second, 3 = third).
- PLAYER CHOICE: markers may be stacked — two or even all three numbered markers may go on the same Army Card.
- All players place simultaneously, markers **face-down/facing the owner** so opponents can't read the numbers. **Teammates MAY look at each other's markers.**
- The **"X" marker is a pure decoy** — place it on any Army Card to bluff; it grants no turn.
- Optional variant for competitive play: instead of simultaneous placement, roll the 20-sided die; the highest roller places ALL of their Order Markers first, then placement passes left until everyone has placed.

### Example 3 (diagram, photos of card rows)
Shows both players' hidden marker assignments for one round:
- You: 1 → Grimnak, 2 → Marro Warriors, 3 → Finn, X → Zettian Guards (bluff).
- Opponent: 1 → Syvarris, 2 AND 3 → Deathwalker (stacking two turns on one card), X → Krav Maga Agents.
Illustrates: stacking markers and decoy usage.

### Step 2 — Roll for Initiative
- ALL players roll the 20-sided die **every round**. Highest roller takes the first turn; play then proceeds **to the left**. Ties among the highest re-roll.
- If a special power or Glyph modifies initiative rolls, the modifier **also applies to re-rolls**.

### Step 3 — On Your Turn
Each player takes 3 turns per round. A turn consists of three actions **in this fixed order**:
- **Action 1. Reveal Your Order Marker** — flip the marker matching the current turn number face-up on its Army Card. If ALL figures on that card were destroyed earlier in the round, the player **loses that turn entirely** (no substitution).
- **Action 2. Move Figure(s) on the Army Card** — PLAYER CHOICE: move any, all, or none of that card's figures.
- **Action 3. Attack with Figure(s) on the Army Card.**

### Action 2 — Movement rules (basics)
- **Move number:** a figure may move in any direction **up to** the Move value on its Army Card (e.g., Move 5 ⇒ may move 1–5 spaces; moving fewer is allowed). Terrain (uneven ground, water) can restrict this.
- **Order of movement:** Squad figures move **one at a time**, in any order the owner chooses.
- **Passing through / landing on figures:**
  - MAY move **through** a space holding a friendly figure (own or teammate's) — **unless that friendly figure is engaged** (see Engagement Rules, page 12).
  - May NOT move through a space holding an **opponent's** figure.
  - May NOT **end** a move on a space occupied by ANY other figure.

### Example 4 (diagram, photo)
Basic movement: Finn (Move 5) advances his full 5 spaces toward enemies, with one step passing **through** a friendly Marro Warrior's space — legal because the Marro is friendly (and not engaged).

## Page 10 — Elevation: moving up, height limits, moving down, falling

### Moving up
- When moving to a higher level, **count the side of each higher level as one space of movement**, in addition to the space landed on. (Climbing costs movement per level.)
- If the figure lacks enough remaining Move to pay for the full climb to the top of a ledge, it **cannot move there at all**.
- **Glyphs and water spaces add NO height** to the spaces they sit on (relevant here and for height advantage on page 14).

### Example 5 (diagram, photo) — Moving up
Shows Finn climbing a two-tier ledge with each counted step numbered; reaching the top ledge would cost a total Move of **7**, but Finn's Move is **5**, so he cannot reach it.

### Height limit for moving up
- A figure may NOT move up, in a single step, a number of levels **equal to or greater than its Height number**. (I.e., a single climb step must rise fewer levels than the figure's Height.)

### Example 6 (diagram, photo) — Height limit
A Marro Warrior (Height **4**) cannot move onto the space marked "X" because that step would rise too many levels relative to its Height.
⚠ UNCLEAR: the exact level count quoted in Example 6's caption (appears to be a 6-space/level climb) is partially illegible; the governing rule (cannot climb ≥ Height levels in one step) is clear.

### Moving down
- Moving to a lower level costs nothing extra — **do NOT count level sides on the way down**. (But large drops can trigger Falling, below.)

### Example 7 (diagram, photo) — Moving down
A Marro Warrior descends a ledge; the path is marked counting only the spaces entered, not the descended sides.

### Falling (automatic check, not optional)
| Situation | Trigger (drop measured in levels) | Resolution |
|---|---|---|
| **Fall** | Drop is greater than the figure's Height | Roll **1 combat die** after moving |
| **Major Fall** | Drop is 10 levels more than the figure's Height | Roll **2 additional dice** (total **3**) |
| **Extreme Fall** | Drop is 20 levels more than the figure's Height | Roll the **20-sided die**: **19–20 = no falling damage; 1–18 = figure destroyed** |

- Each **skull** rolled on falling dice = **1 wound** → place 1 Wound Marker on the figure's Army Card (no defense roll is mentioned for falls). See Life, page 14.
- **Water exception:** falling rules do NOT apply when the figure drops onto a **water space** — a figure may fall onto water from ANY height safely.
⚠ UNCLEAR: whether the Major/Extreme Fall thresholds are "exactly 10/20 more" vs. "10/20 or more levels more than Height" — the small print is ambiguous in the scan; "10+ / 20+ levels more than the figure's Height" is the most consistent reading.

### Example 8 (diagram, photo) — Falling
A Marro Warrior (Height **4**) moves down a **5-level** cliff: the drop exceeds his Height by 1, so 1 attack die is rolled for the fall. No skull comes up, so he takes no wound. Confirms the fall trigger is *drop > Height* (5 > 4).

## Page 11 — Water movement; double-space figures; overhangs

### Moving into water
- Moving **onto a water space** from ANY other space (including from another water space) **immediately ends that figure's move** there.
- **Double-space figure exception:** a double-space figure may pass over a single water space WITHOUT stopping if that water space lies between two non-water spaces; it must stop only when it would occupy **two water spaces at once**.

### Moving from water to land
- Water spaces are often lower than adjacent land; the **"moving up" rule applies** — count the side of each land level climbed as movement when leaving water.

### Example 9A (diagram, photo) — Moving into water
Deathwalker moves onto a water space and must stop there, regardless of remaining Move.

### Example 9B (diagram, photo) — Moving out of water
Moving Deathwalker from a water space to the adjacent (higher) land space costs **2** movement (the land space plus its climbed side).

### Moving double-space figures
- Some figures (e.g., **Grimnak, Mimring**) occupy **two spaces**.
- PLAYER CHOICE: pick which end leads (front or back). The trailing end must then **follow exactly the spaces the leading end just vacated** (snake-style movement).
- A double-space figure must ALWAYS **end its move on two same-level spaces**.

### Example 10 (diagram, 3-photo sequence) — Moving a double-space figure
Grimnak moves 5 spaces head-first; his hind section trails through the same spaces his front section used; counting ledge sides for the climb, he ends atop the ledge on two same-level spaces.

### Overhangs
- An **overhang** exists where one or more tiles sit ABOVE another tile with open space between them.
- Figures **small enough to fit** may move under overhangs; larger figures cannot (physical fit governs legality).

### Example 11 (diagram, photo) — Moving under an overhang
On the "Migol's Tomb" battlefield, **Finn can** move under a pictured overhang but **Grimnak cannot** (too large).

## Page 12 — Low overhangs; base placement; tight quarters; Engagement Rules

### Moving up onto low overhangs
- A figure may climb ONTO a low overhang; when doing so, **count the nearby supporting tile** as part of the climb's movement cost.

### Example 12 (diagram, photo)
Finn counts the adjacent support tile (numbered path shown) while moving up onto a low overhang.

### Keep the base on the space
- At the END of a figure's move, its base must lie **flat and entirely within its space** (or two spaces for double-space figures). No part of the base may overlap another space or the gaps between spaces.

### Tight quarters
- Narrow passages, ruins, and overhangs can physically restrict movement: a figure **cannot move through or onto a space it cannot completely fit on**.

### Example 13 (diagram, 2-photo sequence) — Tight quarters
Grimnak cannot back tail-first into a nook (his tail prevents him standing fully on the spaces), but CAN move into the same nook **forward-facing**. Shows that facing/orientation can determine physical legality.

### Glyphs and special powers (pointer)
- Glyphs and card special powers may also modify movement; check each figure's Army Card. Glyph powers are detailed on page 15.

### ENGAGEMENT RULES
- A figure becomes **engaged** automatically when it moves adjacent to an opponent's figure (and vice versa).
- **Adjacency exceptions** (figures next to each other that are NOT adjacent/engaged):
  1. **Elevation:** if one figure's BASE sits on a level **equal to or higher than the other figure's Height number**, the two are not adjacent and not engaged.
  2. **Ruins between figures:** BOTH figures' Heights must exceed the ruin's height to be engaged across it. If both are shorter than the ruin, or even if only ONE of them is shorter, they are **not engaged**. Worked numbers: ruin height **6** — Finn (Height **5**) and Deathwalker (Height **7**) adjacent across it are NOT engaged (Finn is shorter than 6).

### Example 14 (diagram, photo) — Not adjacent
Finn is NOT adjacent to Deathwalker because Deathwalker stands on a ledge whose level equals Finn's Height of **5** — illustrating exception 1.
⚠ UNCLEAR: the exact ledge level number in Example 14's caption is hard to read (consistent with "5 levels, equal to Finn's Height of 5").

### Leaving an engagement
- You MAY freely move **around** a figure you're engaged with (staying adjacent costs nothing extra).
- The moment your figure moves to a space NOT adjacent to the engaged enemy, that enemy may take a **"passing swipe"** (opponent's option): the controlling player rolls **1 attack die**; a **skull = 1 wound** on your figure (place 1 Wound Marker on its Army Card). No defense roll is mentioned — the swipe is unblockable.

### Multiple engagements
- A figure can be engaged with several enemies simultaneously. When it disengages, **EACH engaged enemy** gets its own passing swipe (1 die each).

## Page 13 — Multiple-engagement example; Action 3: Attack — who attacks, Range, Line of Sight

### Example 15 (diagram, photo + 2 skull-die icons) — Multiple engagement
Finn moves away from TWO opposing Tarn Viking Warriors; the opponent rolls **2 dice** (one per Viking). Both show skulls ⇒ **2 wounds** ⇒ 2 Wound Markers on Finn's Army Card.

### Action 3 — Attack with Figure(s) on the Army Card
- **Who can attack:** any figure(s) on the active Army Card that have BOTH the target **within Range** AND a **clear Line of Sight**. If no figure on the card meets both conditions, no attack happens that turn.
- **Looking for hidden figures:** players may physically walk around the table at any time to see what's hiding behind ruins/terrain. (Other players may shield their Order Markers while you do.)

### Range
- The targeted figure must be within the attacker's **Range** number, counted in spaces along the battlefield (e.g., Range 6 ⇒ targets up to 6 spaces away).
- **Range 1** ⇒ may only attack adjacent figures (melee).
- **Engaged figures' restriction:** a figure engaged with one or more enemies may attack **ONLY those engaged figures** (cannot shoot past its engagement).
- A **double-space figure** may measure its attack **from either of its two spaces**.
- Vertical distance is FREE for Range: do NOT count extra spaces for level changes (higher ground instead grants Height Advantage dice — page 14).
- **Gaps with no spaces** (e.g., over a board edge/chasm): you may still attack across them, but Range must be counted **along actual battlefield spaces** (around the gap), not as the crow flies.
- **Attacking around other figures:** intervening figures do NOT block an attack by themselves — only Line of Sight matters.

### Line of Sight (LoS)
- The attacker must be able to "see" the target. LoS is an **imaginary straight line** — unrelated to spaces/Range counting — from the attacker's **Target Point** (marked as a **green dot** on its Army Card silhouette) to ANY part of the target's **Hit Zone** (the **red area** on the target's Army Card silhouette).
- Procedure: physically get behind/at the attacking figure's Target Point and check whether any part of the target's Hit Zone is visible. If yes ⇒ clear LoS; if blocked (e.g., by a ruin or cliff) ⇒ no attack possible.
- **Adjusting figures:** players may touch/reposition figures ONLY on their own turns — you cannot nudge your figure out of LoS during an opponent's turn.
- **Dispute resolution:** if players disagree about LoS, all roll the 20-sided die; **highest roller wins the dispute** (no further argument).

### Example 16 (diagram, 2 photos) — Range and Line of Sight
A Marro Warrior wants to attack Syvarris:
1. Count spaces between them: **6**, so Syvarris is within the Marro's Range (6).
2. Sight from the Marro's Target Point to Syvarris' Hit Zone: a Tarn Viking stands partially in the way, but **part of the Hit Zone is still visible**, so LoS is clear and the attack may proceed. (Partial cover does not matter — ANY visible part of the Hit Zone suffices.)

## Page 14 — Attacking procedure; height advantage; Life & destruction; round/battle end; scoring

### Attacking — general
- The active card's figures attack **one at a time, in any order** (owner's choice).
- Each figure attacks **once per turn** (unless a special power says otherwise); multiple figures MAY pile onto the same target.
- Before each attack, the attacker should consider height advantage, special powers, and Glyphs — the rulebook applies a "you snooze, you lose" convention: forgotten bonuses are **not retroactive**.

### Height Advantage
- If one figure's **base** is on a higher level than the other figure's base (actual model size irrelevant), the higher figure gets **+1 die** (applies to BOTH attacking and defending, per the steps below).
- If the higher figure's base is **10 or more levels above the lower figure's Height**, it gets **+2 dice** instead.
- Reminder: **Glyphs and water do not add height** to a space.

### Special powers / special attacks
- Army Cards may carry special powers affecting move/attack/defense/etc. If a card lists a **special attack**, the player may CHOOSE to use it **instead of** a normal attack. Warning printed: some special powers/attacks can harm **friendly figures** too. (Examples on pages 15–16.)

### Glyphs
- Some Glyphs grant attacker or defender advantages; detailed on page 15.

### Attack resolution steps (per attack)
1. **Announce** the attacking figure and the defending figure.
2. Attacker rolls a number of **attack dice = card's Attack number + extra dice** (height advantage / special powers / Glyphs). Defender then rolls **defense dice = card's Defense number + extra dice** (same possible bonuses).
3. Count **only skulls** on attack dice (each skull = 1 hit) and **only shields** on defense dice; each shield blocks one skull.
   - If shields **≥** skulls ⇒ attack fails; nothing happens, attack over (no retreat/knockback).
   - If skulls **>** shields ⇒ for **each unblocked skull**, place **1 Wound Marker** on the defender's Army Card.

### Life and destruction
- A figure is **destroyed** when Wound Markers on its card **equal its Life number** (Life 1 ⇒ dies to a single wound).
- The destroyed figure's owner places the miniature **on its Army Card**.
- When ALL figures on an Army Card are destroyed, the card is **out of play**:
  - Unrevealed Order Markers on it are **not revealed** this round, and any turn assigned to it is **lost** (matches page 9, Action 1).
  - In future rounds no turns may be taken for that card, and its Order Markers **return to the player's pool** for placement on other cards.
- After the active card finishes attacking, the turn ends; play passes **to the left**.

### Example 17 (diagram, photos + card with wound markers) — An attack
- A Marro Warrior attacks Syvarris: attacker rolls **2 attack dice** (Marro Attack = 2, no bonuses).
- Syvarris has minor height advantage ⇒ adds **+1 die** to his Defense number of 2 ⇒ rolls **3 defense dice**.
- Result: 2 skulls vs. 0 shields ⇒ **2 wounds** ⇒ 2 Wound Markers placed.
- Syvarris already had 2 Wound Markers from earlier ⇒ now **4 total = his Life** ⇒ destroyed. Opponent places the Syvarris figure on its Army Card; an Order Marker still on the card stays and **that turn is skipped** this round.
- Implied card data: Syvarris — Life **4**, Defense **2** (3 dice including the +1); Marro Warrior — Attack **2**.

### Ending a Round
- After the LAST player's THIRD turn, the round ends. Advance the Round Marker one space (if no one has won), then start the next round: place Order Markers → roll initiative.

### Ending the Battle
- Victory conditions come from the **Game Scenario**. Optional house-timer rule: if the game hasn't ended after an agreed number of rounds, players MAY decide the player/side with the most points wins (see Scoring).

### Scoring
- When scoring is needed: each player/team scores points for **each Army Card with at least one figure still on the battlefield**, scoring the **FULL point value printed on the card even if only one Squad figure survives** (no pro-rating).

## Key terms introduced
- **Army / Army Card** — a unit's stat card; an Army is a set of cards within the scenario point limit.
- **Pre-made Army vs. Drafting** — the two army-selection methods; drafting alternates picks with immediate placement.
- **Starting zone** — designated board area where a player's entire Army must deploy.
- **Order Markers (1, 2, 3, X)** — secret per-round turn scheduling tokens; "X" is a no-effect decoy.
- **Round / Round Marker / Round Marker Track** — a round = 3 turns per player; the marker tracks round count.
- **Initiative** — per-round d20 roll determining first player; play passes left.
- **Move number** — max spaces a figure may move on its turn.
- **Height number** — a figure's physical height in levels; governs climbing limits, adjacency exceptions, and fall thresholds.
- **Moving up / Moving down** — climbing costs 1 move per level side; descending is free.
- **Falling / Major Fall / Extreme Fall** — damage tiers for large drops (1 die / 3 dice / d20 survival check).
- **Double-space figure** — figure occupying two hexes; trailing end follows the leader; must end on two same-level spaces.
- **Overhang / Tight quarters** — physical-fit movement restrictions.
- **Engaged / Engagement** — state of being adjacent to an enemy (with elevation and ruin exceptions); restricts attack targets.
- **Passing swipe** — free 1-die unblockable attack against a figure leaving engagement; one per engaged enemy.
- **Range** — max attack distance in spaces (counted along the board; vertical levels free).
- **Line of Sight (LoS)** — straight visual line from Target Point to any part of the Hit Zone.
- **Target Point** — green dot on the attacker's card silhouette (where LoS is drawn FROM).
- **Hit Zone** — red region on the target's card silhouette (what must be visible).
- **Height Advantage** — +1 die (or +2 if 10+ levels above the lower figure's Height) for the figure on the higher base.
- **Attack / Defense numbers** — base dice counts for combat rolls; skulls hit, shields block.
- **Wound Marker / Life** — damage tracking; wounds = Life ⇒ figure destroyed.
- **Special attack** — optional replacement for a normal attack, printed on some cards.
- **Glyph** — board tile granting powers to figures that land on it (detailed page 15); adds no height.
- **Scoring** — full card points for any card with a surviving figure.
