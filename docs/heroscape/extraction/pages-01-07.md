## Page 4 — Basic Game Guide: setup, turn structure, card choice, basic movement

### Objective
Build a battlefield, each player chooses an Army, then fight; the first player to achieve the **victory objective of the chosen Game Scenario** wins (objectives vary per scenario — not fixed "kill everything").

### Setup
1. **Battlefield + scenario:** Use the Battlefield & Game Scenario section (p. 17) — five battlefields with step-by-step build instructions; Basic Game Scenarios each define their own victory conditions. Setup is scenario-driven, not freeform, for new players.
2. **Armies (Basic Game):** Armies are pre-assigned, not drafted — **Player 1 takes the Good Army, Player 2 takes the Evil Army** (composition per scenario). Each player places their Army Cards face-up in front of them and puts their figures on the scenario's starting positions.
3. **Cards have two sides:** Basic Game uses the Basic side of each Army Card (only Move/Range/Attack/Defense); the Master side is for the Master Game.
4. **Card types:** A **Hero Card** = exactly one (usually powerful) figure. A **Squad Card** = two or more (usually weaker) figures that act together as one card.
5. **Dice:** Place the 12 combat dice by the board. The same dice serve as **attack dice (count skulls)** and **defense dice (count shields)**. No other components are used in the Basic Game unless the scenario says so.

### First player and turn order
Each player rolls combat dice; **the player rolling the most skulls goes first; re-roll ties.** Players then strictly alternate turns until the game ends.

⚠ UNCLEAR: The numeral for how many combat dice each player rolls for first-turn determination could not be resolved at scan resolution (appears to be a single digit, likely 6). Verify before implementing.

### Turn structure (Basic Game) — always in this order
1. **Action 1 — Choose one Army Card** (player choice, any one of your cards). Only the figure(s) on that card act this turn.
2. **Action 2 — Move figure(s) on that card** (optional, player choice per figure).
3. **Action 3 — Attack with figure(s) on that card** (covered on pp. 5–6).

A sidebar explicitly frames card choice as the core decision (positions, stats, target, scenario objective) — advisory text, no rule content.

### Basic movement rules (Action 2)
- **Distance:** Each figure on the chosen card may move **0 up to its Move number** of spaces, in any direction (no facing). E.g., Move 4 → 1, 2, 3, or 4 spaces. Terrain (elevation, water) can effectively reduce this.
- **Order:** Squad figures move **one at a time, in any order the controller chooses**; a figure's move is completed before the next starts.
- **Passing through figures:** May move **through spaces occupied by friendly figures**; may **never move through spaces occupied by enemy figures**.
- **Ending movement:** May **never end a move on any occupied space** (friendly or enemy).

## Page 5 — Basic Game Guide: elevation/water movement, double-space figures, attack eligibility

### Elevation
- **Moving UP:** When moving onto a higher-level space, count **+1 space for each level side climbed, in addition to the destination space itself**. (So stepping up one level onto an adjacent hex costs 2 movement; up two levels costs 3; etc.)
- **Moving DOWN:** Descending is free — moving to any lower-level space costs only the destination space (1), regardless of how many levels are dropped.
- Example 4 (diagram): a Zettian Guard spends all 4 movement to climb onto a ledge (sides counted as spaces on the way up).
- Example 5 (diagram): the same ledge descended costs only 1 space.

### Water (Basic Game)
- **Entering water:** Moving onto a water space **from any space — including from another water space — immediately ends that figure's move** (automatic, not optional). So a figure in water moves at most 1 space per turn while in water.
- Example 6A (diagram): a Guard with Move 4 enters water on its 2nd space and must stop there.
- **Leaving water onto higher land:** Water spaces are lower than adjacent land, so the **moving-up rule applies**: count the side of each land level climbed when stepping out.
- Example 6B (diagram): stepping from water onto the adjacent land space costs 2 (side + landing space).

### Double-space figures (e.g., Grimnak, Mimring — figures occupying 2 hexes)
- **Leading end (player choice):** Choose which end leads (front or back). The figure then moves like a snake: the **trailing end must follow exactly through the spaces the leading end just vacated**.
- **End of move constraint:** Must always end its move occupying **two spaces of the same level**.
- **Water exception:** A double-space figure does **NOT** have to stop when passing over a single water space lying between two land spaces (one end bridges over it), **but MUST stop when moving down onto two adjacent water spaces** (i.e., when both of its spaces would be in water).
- Example 7 (3-panel diagram): Mimring moves 6 spaces head-first; back section traces the head's path; he counts a land-space side when stepping up out of water mid-path; he ends straddling across water on two same-level spaces.

### Action 3 — Attack: who may attack
- Each figure on the chosen Army Card may attack **only if BOTH conditions hold**: target enemy figure is **within the attacker's Range**, AND attacker has a **clear Line of Sight** to it.
- If **no** figure on the chosen card satisfies both conditions against any enemy, no attack occurs and the turn simply ends.

## Page 6 — Basic Game Guide: range, line of sight, attack resolution, height advantage, game end

### Range (space-counting check)
- Target must be within the attacker's Range counted **in board spaces** from attacker to target. Range 7 → any enemy within 7 spaces.
- **Range 1 = melee:** can only attack a figure on an **adjacent** space.
- **Double-space figures measure Range from either of their two occupied spaces** (attacker's choice of better end).
- **Elevation is FREE for Range** — do not count level sides when measuring Range (unlike movement). Height matters only via Height Advantage (below).
- **No measuring across gaps:** if a straight path would cross an area with no spaces (e.g., past the board edge), Range must be counted along actual battlefield spaces around the gap.

### Line of Sight (visual check, separate from Range)
- LOS is an **imaginary straight line through 3-D space** — it ignores spaces/hexes entirely (explicitly contrasted with Range).
- Method given: physically look from **behind the attacking figure's head** toward the target. If **any part** of the target figure is visible, LOS is clear.
- If the target is fully hidden (e.g., behind a ruin or a terrain ledge), no attack is possible.
- LOS **may pass beyond the board edge** (over areas with no spaces) — that does not block it; only physical obstructions block.
- Example 8 (diagram): Zettian Guard vs. Airborne Elite — step 1 counts exactly 7 spaces (just inside Range 7); step 2 sights from behind the Guard's head and sees the top of the target's figure → attack allowed. Illustrates that partial visibility (head/top only) suffices.

### Attack resolution (Basic Game)
Figures on the active card attack **one at a time, in any order; each figure attacks at most once per turn; multiple figures may attack the same defender.** Per attack:
1. **Declare** (attacker player): which of your figures attacks and which enemy figure is the target (the "Defender").
2. **Roll:** Attacker rolls dice equal to the attacker's **Attack** number, counting **only skulls**. Then the defending player rolls dice equal to the Defender's **Defense** number, counting **only shields**. (Skulls on defense dice and shields on attack dice are ignored.)
   - **Height Advantage:** if one figure's **base** is on a higher level than the other's (figure sculpt size is irrelevant — base elevation only), that figure rolls **+1 die** on its roll. Applies symmetrically: higher attacker rolls +1 attack die; higher defender rolls +1 defense die.
3. **Compare:**
   - **skulls > shields → Defender is destroyed** (Basic Game has no wounds/Life — destruction is all-or-nothing). The Defender's controller removes it from the game.
   - **skulls ≤ shields → attack fails, nothing happens** (no partial damage, no knockback; ties favor the defender).

After every figure on the card that can (and that the player wishes to) attack has done so, the turn is over.

### Example 9 (worked combat, Zettian Guards vs. Airborne Elite)
- First attack: Attack 2, +1 die for height advantage = **3 attack dice → 2 skulls**. Defender: Defense 2 = **2 defense dice → 2 shields**. 2 ≤ 2 → blocked, no effect.
- Second attack (other Guard, same Defender, also has height advantage): **3 attack dice → 2 skulls**; defender **2 dice → 1 shield**. 2 > 1 → Defender destroyed and removed.
- Confirms: per-figure height advantage evaluation, multiple attackers on one target, tie-goes-to-defender, binary destruction.

### Ending the battle
Play continues until a player fulfills the chosen scenario's **Victory objective** — that player immediately wins. Closing text encourages custom battlefields/armies/scenarios later (no rules).

⚠ UNCLEAR: The combat dice face distribution (how many skulls/shields/blanks per d6) is never stated on pages 1–7 — only that attackers count skulls and defenders count shields. Expect it later in the book or determine empirically (standard HeroScape dice are 3 skulls / 2 shields / 1 blank, but that is NOT confirmed by these pages).

## Page 7 — Master Game Guide: setup, team play, Army Card anatomy (Master side)

### Framing
Master Game = the full game: same core as Basic, but figures use the Master side of their cards, gaining **Life (wounds), special powers, point values, and extra movement/combat rules**. Players are styled "Valkyrie Generals" who assemble their own armies.

### Setup
- **Objective:** as Basic — first to achieve the chosen scenario's objective wins.
- Choose a battlefield + **Master Game Scenario** from the section starting p. 17; custom battlefields/scenarios encouraged after a few games.
- **Team play (any team scenario):** teammates sit together on one side facing opponents; each teammate **plays their own separate game turns** but **open table-talk/strategy discussion among teammates is allowed**. Team victory conditions vary by scenario but are usually a shared common goal.
- **Multiple Master Sets and/or Expansion Sets:** special combining rules exist, deferred to **p. 16**.

### Army Cards (Master side) — field-by-field
- **Unique vs. Common:** every card/figure in this Master Set is **Unique — only one copy of each may exist**. Common cards/figures (duplicates allowed) only appear in expansion sets.
- **Hero vs. Squad:** as Basic (Hero = 1 figure; Squad = 2+ figures). **Army construction is unrestricted by type**: all Heroes, all Squads, or any mix.
- **Valkyrie (General):** which of the five Generals the card belongs to — **Jandar, Utgar, Ullar, Vydar, or Einar**. (Army-building implications presumably later.)
- **Species:** the figure's race (e.g., Human).
- **Class:** figure's type/profession (e.g., Agent; card header also carries Unique Hero designation).
- **Personality:** dominant character trait (e.g., Tricky) — flavor/synergy hook.
- **Size/Height:** size class plus a number of levels tall (e.g., **Medium 5** = medium size, 5 levels high). Height number interacts with LOS/elevation rules.
- **Life:** number of **wounds the figure can take before being destroyed** (Master Game replaces Basic's one-hit destruction with wound tracking).
- **Move / Range / Attack / Defense:** same meanings as Basic (max spaces; attack distance in spaces; attack dice; defense dice).
- **Points:** the card's army-building cost/value, based on figure power.
- **Special Power(s):** named abilities, unique per card, that explicitly **"break the rules"** for moving, attacking, or defending — card text overrides general rules.
- **Target Point (green dot on card silhouette):** the point on the figure from which Line of Sight is determined.
- **Hit Zone (red area on card silhouette):** the body region that counts when checking LOS — only seeing the Hit Zone allows an attack (Master Game tightens Basic's "see any part" rule).

## Key terms introduced

- **Army / Army Card** — a player's force; each card is one Hero or one Squad plus its figure(s).
- **Hero Card** — Army Card with exactly one figure.
- **Squad Card** — Army Card with two or more figures that activate together.
- **Basic Game side / Master Game side** — the two faces of every Army Card (stats only vs. full data + powers).
- **Move / Range / Attack / Defense** — the four core stats (spaces movable; attack distance in spaces; attack dice; defense dice).
- **Combat dice** — shared d6 pool; read as **skulls** when attacking, **shields** when defending.
- **Attacker / Defender** — declared roles within a single attack.
- **Height Advantage** — +1 die to whichever combatant's base sits on the higher level.
- **Line of Sight (LOS)** — straight visual line independent of spaces; required (with Range) to attack.
- **Double-space figure** — figure occupying two hexes; leading-end movement, same-level ending rule.
- **Engagement / Disengage** — adjacency-based combat state (only previewed here via Agent Carr's power; full rule later).
- **Unique / Common** — one-copy-only cards vs. duplicable expansion cards.
- **Valkyrie General** — army faction leader: Jandar, Utgar, Ullar, Vydar, Einar.
- **Species / Class / Personality / Size-Height** — Master-side classification fields (synergy hooks).
- **Life** — wound capacity (Master Game).
- **Points** — army-building cost of a card.
- **Special Power** — card-specific rule that overrides general rules.
- **Target Point / Hit Zone** — green dot (LOS origin) and red zone (attackable region) on the Master-side card silhouette.
- **Glyph / Wound marker / Order marker / Round marker / Grenade marker** — components named in the manifest; rules for them not yet given on pages 1–7.
- **Ruins** — physical wall pieces that block Line of Sight.
