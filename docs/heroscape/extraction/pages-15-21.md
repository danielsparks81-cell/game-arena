## Page 15 — Master Game Guide: Glyphs & Special Powers

### Glyphs — general rules
- Which Glyphs appear, and where, depends on the Scenario. Glyphs sit on battlefield spaces and are **stationary** unless a scenario says otherwise.
- A Glyph can start the game either **symbol-side up** or **power-side up** (the scenario's battlefield diagram dictates which).
- **Trigger (symbol-side up):** when a figure a player controls moves onto a symbol-side-up Glyph, that figure must **stop its movement immediately**, the Glyph is flipped power-side up, and its power takes effect immediately. This is automatic, not optional, once the player chooses to move onto the space.
- **Trigger (power-side up):** any figure that moves onto a power-side-up Glyph must also stop there (its power applies per the rules below).
- Double-space (two-hex) figures: moving onto a Glyph forces the stop as well.
  - ⚠ UNCLEAR: the exact wording of the double-space note (which of the figure's two spaces triggers the stop) is too small to read with certainty.
- Glyph powers come in two duration classes: **Permanent** (lasts while occupied) and **Temporary** (one-shot).

### Permanent Glyphs
- A permanent Glyph's power is in effect **only for as long as a figure is standing on it**, and it benefits the controller of the occupying figure (it applies to ALL figures that player controls, not just the occupant, where the table below says "each figure you control").
- **Exception:** the two Glyphs of Brandar are *Artifacts* — they represent scenario-specific objects and follow the scenario's rules instead.

| Glyph | Power | Effect while one of your figures occupies it |
|---|---|---|
| Glyph of Astrid | Attack +1 | Every figure you control rolls 1 extra attack die |
| Glyph of Gerda | Defense +1 | Every figure you control rolls 1 extra defense die |
| Glyph of Ivor | Range +4 | Every figure you control **whose Range is 4 or more** adds 4 to its Range number |
| Glyph of Valda | Move +2 | Every figure you control adds 2 to its Move number. **Exception:** the bonus does NOT apply to the occupying figure's move that takes it off the Glyph |
| Glyph of Dagmar | Initiative +8 | When you roll for initiative, add 8 to your d20 roll |
| Glyph of Brandar (×2) | Artifact | No fixed power — rules vary per Game Scenario |

### Temporary Glyphs
- A temporary Glyph's power fires **exactly once**: when one of your figures moves onto it, resolve its rule, then **remove the Glyph from the game**.

| Glyph | Power | Effect (one-time, on stopping there) |
|---|---|---|
| Glyph of Kelda | Heal | Only a figure carrying **1 or more Wound Markers** is allowed to stop on this Glyph. When one of your figures stops here, remove **all** Wound Markers from its Army Card. This Glyph may never be placed symbol-side up in player-created scenarios |
| Glyph of Erland | Summoning | The moving player **may choose** to "summon" any other figure on the battlefield — friendly **or** enemy — to the Glyph (placed adjacent). The summoned figure does **not** suffer a leaving-engagement attack even if it was engaged. If there is no empty adjacent space, the Summon power cannot be used |
| Glyph of Mitonsoul | Massive Curse | For **every figure on the battlefield (all players')**, roll the 20-sided die once per figure: on a **1** that figure is destroyed; on **2–20** it is safe. Automatic, affects friend and foe alike |

### Special Powers (Army Card powers) — general rulings
- Every figure's card special powers are always in effect per their card text; players are reminded to track opponents' powers.
- **Special Attack rule:** special attacks can **never** be modified — no bonuses from Glyphs, from other special powers, or from height advantage.
- **"Attack dice" / "defense dice" on cards = combat dice.** Example given: Raelin's aura grants "+2 defense dice" to all figures her controller controls within 4 clear-sight spaces — this means those figures roll 2 additional *combat dice* when defending.
- **Multiple targets:** when a special attack hits more than one figure, the **attacker chooses the order** in which the defenders resolve their defense rolls. Exception: Mimring's Fire Line special attack (order is fixed by distance — see below).
- **Clear sight spaces** (used by Raelin's Defensive Aura): determined from **Raelin's Target Point**; a friendly figure is in the aura if **any part of the figure** (not merely its Hit Zone) can be seen within the 4 spaces.

### Mimring's Fire Line Special Attack
- The fire line is traced from **either Mimring's front space or his back space** (he is a double-space figure), extending in a straight line; figures within **8 spaces** along the line are affected (red lines in the diagram).
- Affected figures may be on **any elevation level**, provided Mimring has clear line of sight to them.
- Resolution order is **automatic, not attacker's choice**: the figure closest along the line to Mimring rolls defense first, then the next closest, and so on.
- **Fire Line into an overhang:** the attack affects figures on **all levels** within the line, as long as Mimring can see them. In the diagram example, Airborne Elite figures A1, A2 and A3 (standing at different elevations under/on an overhang) are all hit; Mimring rolls his **4 attack dice separately against each affected figure**.

## Page 16 — Master Game Guide: Flying over Overhangs, Lob/Grenade Special-Attack Example, Multi-Set Rules

### Flying and overhangs (worked example with diagram)
- A flying figure (Raelin in the diagram) flies out from under an overhang up onto its top, **ignoring the extra movement cost of elevation changes** (flying counts spaces, not levels).
- Leaving-engagement attacks: a Marro figure that Raelin was **engaged with when she started her move** gets a leaving-engagement attack against her; another Marro that she merely **flies over without landing** does NOT get one.
  - ⚠ UNCLEAR: the specific figure labels (M1 vs M3) in this example are too small to read reliably; the rule distinction (engaged-at-start ⇒ attack; flown-over ⇒ no attack) is clear.

### The Airborne Elite Lob Grenades (worked special-attack example)
- Grenade special attack mechanics shown by example:
  - Using the power **removes the Grenade Marker from the game** (one use for the squad).
  - Targets: all figures within **Range 5** and a **lob height of 12 levels or less**.
  - Height check example: a target on top of an overhang cannot be attacked because overhang height + a 6-level ruin on top totals **16 levels**, exceeding the 12-level lob limit. (Implies the overhang itself is 10 levels in this example.)
  - **Line of sight is NOT required** to lob a grenade — only a clear area to lob into (height clearance).
- Resolution example (4 Airborne Elite figures A1–A4 attacking Samurai S1–S3 and Marro M1–M4). Mechanical takeaways:
  - A grenade targets one figure; **all figures adjacent to the target are also affected**.
  - The attacker rolls the grenade's attack dice (2 dice in the example) **once**, and that single result applies to every affected figure.
  - Each affected figure's owner then rolls **its own defense separately** against that one attack result; any figure whose shields are fewer than the skulls rolled is wounded/destroyed.
  - If the attack roll yields **0 skulls, no defense rolls are needed** — all affected figures are automatically safe.
  - Each attacking squad figure lobs its own grenade as a separate attack (A1, A2, A3, A4 attack in sequence in the example; one rolls 1 skull, one rolls 0 skulls, one rolls 2 skulls, etc.).
  - ⚠ UNCLEAR: the exact shield counts in two lines of the example (which Samurai/Marro survived with how many shields) are partially illegible; the procedure above is what the example demonstrates.

### Special power references on Army Cards
- Card powers may reference other figures' **Species, Personality, Valkyrie (General), Unique/Common status, Hero/Squad status, and/or Class**.
- Some referenced figures exist in this Master Set (example: Deathwalker references Soulborg Guards — the Zettian Guards qualify); others only exist in expansion sets (example: Grimnak references Orc Warriors, which appear in one or more expansion sets).

### Simultaneous special powers
- If two opposing players field the **same Army Card** and its power would trigger for both **at the same time**, the players roll the **20-sided die**; the winner uses/resolves the power first. Example: both players have Airborne Elite — roll off to see whose "The Drop" resolves first.

### Using multiple Master Sets and/or Expansion Sets
- Combining sets allows: bigger battlefields, more players, mirror-matched armies, and larger army point totals.
- **Unique Army Cards:** every Army Card in a Master Set is Unique (only one exists per set).
  - One player's army may **never** contain two copies of the same Unique Army Card (e.g., you can't field two Grimnaks).
  - It IS legal for **two different players** to each field the same Unique card (each player may have one Grimnak).
- **Common Army Cards** (found in expansion sets): one army MAY include multiple copies of the same Common Army Card, with these rules:
  - Figures from duplicate Common cards are **interchangeable** — you do not track which physical figure belongs to which card. Example: with two Blade Gruts cards, an order marker placed on either card activates **any 4** Blade Gruts figures.
  - When a Common-card figure is destroyed, place it on any matching Common Army Card — but you must **completely fill one card with destroyed figures before starting to fill another** (no spreading casualties across cards).
- **Marking figures:** when mixing multiple sets, players are advised to initial the underside of their figures' bases with a permanent marker (ownership tracking; advisory, not a rule).

### The Tiles — physical assembly
- Hex tiles come in the sizes/colors above; battlefields are built by **linking** (sliding a tile's notches into another tile's grooves — explicitly *slide*, do not snap/press them together) and **stacking** (seating one tile flat on top of another). ## Key terms introduced

- **Glyph** — an enchantment tile placed on a battlefield space; symbol-side up (dormant) or power-side up (active); landing on one forces a movement stop.
- **Symbol-side up / Power-side up** — the two orientations of a Glyph; moving onto a symbol-side-up Glyph flips it and activates it.
- **Permanent Glyph** — a Glyph whose power lasts as long as a figure stands on it (Astrid, Gerda, Ivor, Valda, Dagmar).
- **Temporary Glyph** — a Glyph whose power triggers once, then it is removed from the game (Kelda, Erland, Mitonsoul).
- **Artifact (Glyph of Brandar)** — a Glyph standing in for a scenario-specific object whose rules come from the scenario (e.g., the deep-dive devices in "Dive the Dark Lakes").
- **Wound Marker** — damage token on an Army Card; all removed by the Glyph of Kelda.
- **Summon** — Glyph of Erland effect: relocate any figure (friend or foe) to the Glyph's vicinity without a leaving-engagement attack.
- **Special Attack** — a card-granted attack that can never be modified by Glyphs, other special powers, or height advantage.
- **Combat dice** — the physical dice used whenever a card says "attack dice" or "defense dice".
- **Clear sight spaces** — range measurement for auras (e.g., Raelin's): seeing any part of a figure (not only its Hit Zone) from the source's Target Point counts.
- **Target Point / Hit Zone** — sighting reference points on a figure (Target Point used for clear-sight checks; Hit Zone is the narrower attack-sighting zone defined earlier in the rulebook).
- **Fire Line Special Attack** — Mimring's straight-line, 8-space, multi-level attack; defenders resolve closest-first.
- **Lob (grenade) attack** — Airborne Elite special attack: Range 5, max lob height 12 levels, no line of sight required, splash to figures adjacent to the target, one attack roll defended separately by each affected figure.
- **Leaving-engagement attack** — free attack against a figure that starts its move engaged; not triggered by flying over an enemy without landing.
- **Unique Army Card** — only one copy allowed per player's army; opposing players may each field the same Unique card.
- **Common Army Card** — expansion-set card; multiple copies allowed in one army, figures interchangeable across matching cards, destroyed figures fill one card before the next.
- **Order marker (on Common cards)** — activates the card's figure count drawn from any matching figures (e.g., any 4 Blade Gruts).
- **The Drop** — Airborne Elite special power; named here as (a) example of simultaneous-power roll-off and (b) banned for Winter Holdout reinforcements.
- **Battlefield Key** — tile-identification chart: center color = terrain (grass/sand/rock/water), border color = size (tan 24, purple 7, black 3, yellow 2, red/blue 1).
- **Linking / Stacking** — the two physical operations for assembling terrain (slide notches into grooves; seat tiles on top of others).
- **Ruin (long/short)** — wall terrain pieces placed in the final build step, drawn as red zigzags in diagrams.
- **Starting zone** — colored map region (brown vs navy in these scenarios) where a player's/team's army deploys.
- **Round Marker Track** — 1–12 track used to count rounds; Master Games end after Round 12 with a points-on-battlefield tiebreak.
- **Reinforcements** — Winter Holdout special rule: end-of-round d20 roll (16+ after Rounds 1–2, 11+ later) to deploy a one-time 160-point force in your original starting zone.
- **Draft / pre-made army** — the two ways to build a scenario army to its point budget (player choice).
- **Deep-dive device** — Basic-Game objective object represented by the Brandar Glyphs in "Dive the Dark Lakes".
- **Archkyrie** — lore term for the warring Valkyrie generals of Laur and Nostralund (flavor only).
