# HeroScape Rulebook Extraction — Pages 22–28

Source: scanned rulebook pages (`C:\Users\Dan\Desktop\heroscape-extract\img\page-22.png` … `page-28.png`).
These pages contain NO core-rules text. They are the back-of-book content: three battlefield build guides and six ready-to-play scenarios, plus publisher back matter. Scenario pages each carry a printed "Round Marker Track" sidebar (a physical track where the round marker is advanced).

Common scenario structure on these pages: flavor paragraph → **Goal** → **Setup** (glyph placement, army points, start zones) → **Special Rules** → **Victory**, plus a map diagram showing color-coded starting zones and glyph/figure positions.

## Page 23 — Durgeth Swamps Scenarios

Left column finishes the Durgeth build: **Level 08** diagram and a **Ruins** diagram (where the ruin wall pieces stand). Right margin: **Round Marker Track numbered 1–12**.

### BASIC GAME — "The Search for Comfrey Plants" (2 players)
Flavor summary: heroes of Jandar and Utgar race to gather healing comfrey plants for their wounded.

- **Goal:** be the first player to find 2 comfrey plants.
- **Setup (hidden-objective mechanic):**
  - The 2 Glyphs of Brandar plus the Glyph of Kelda represent hidden comfrey plants (3 plants total).
  - ALL glyphs are turned symbol-side up (face down / power hidden), shuffled, and placed on the map spaces marked "?". The non-plant glyphs are decoys.
  - Fixed preset armies (the Basic Game armies), placed at numbered start positions shown on the map:

| Player | Figures (placement order 1–3) |
|---|---|
| Player 1 | Thorgrim the Viking Champion; Sgt. Drake Alexander; Syvarris |
| Player 2 | Ne-Gok-Sa; Deathwalker 9000; Agent Carr |

- **Special Rules (automatic, triggers on entering a glyph space):** when one of your figures lands on a glyph it MUST stop there and the glyph is turned over.
  - If it is NOT a comfrey plant (i.e., not Brandar or Kelda): remove that glyph from the battlefield (decoy glyphs' powers never take effect).
  - If it IS a comfrey plant: the player keeps it power-side up in front of them (collected).
- **Victory (two alternative conditions):** first player to collect 2 of the 3 comfrey plants wins; OR you win immediately if the opponent has no figures left on the battlefield.

⚠ UNCLEAR: the exact count of "?" spaces on the map diagram (i.e., how many decoy glyphs are seeded along with the 3 plants) could not be counted reliably at this resolution.

### MASTER GAME — "Under Tempest's Cover" (2 players)
Hidden-identity escort scenario. Flavor summary: a storm rages; an enemy scout must carry intelligence across the swamp to a transfer post before time runs out.

- **Player 1's Goal:** get your secret Hero Scout through Durgeth Swamps onto the Valkyrie's transfer post by the end of Round 12.
- **Player 2's Goal:** prevent that.
- **Setup:**
  - Glyph of Gerda placed power-side up where shown (its normal power is active).
  - A Glyph of Brandar placed power-side up where shown — it represents the Valkyrie's transfer post (destination marker).
  - Each player drafts or brings a pre-made **400-point** army.
  - Hidden scout assignment (Player 1 choice, secret): Player 1 takes one glyph per Unique Hero Army Card he controls — one of which must be the Glyph of Kelda — and secretly places one glyph symbol-side up on each of those cards. The Unique Hero whose card holds the Glyph of Kelda is the secret Hero Scout.
  - Start zones: Player 1 = brown zone; Player 2 = navy zone.
- **Special Rules:**
  - Storm: no flying and no parachuting in this scenario. Army Cards with "The Drop" special power ignore that power and are placed like normal figures.
  - Round 1 only: Player 2 adds **+12** to his initiative roll (effectively guaranteeing first initiative).
  - Information reveal (automatic trigger): whenever a Unique Hero controlled by Player 1 is destroyed — or control of it is taken by Player 2 — the glyph on that hero's Army Card is turned over (revealing whether that hero was the scout).
- **Victory:**
  - Player 1 wins immediately if he moves a Unique Hero onto the transfer post AND it is the scout (Glyph of Kelda revealed from its card).
  - Player 2 wins if the scout is destroyed.
  - Player 2 also wins if the scout has not reached the transfer post by the end of Round 12 — even if Player 2 has no figures left on the battlefield (a timeout win does not require survival).

## Page 25 — Trollsford Swamps Scenarios

Right margin: **Round Marker Track numbered 1–8** (both scenarios on this page are 8-round games).

### MASTER GAME — "A Desperate Rescue Attempt" (3 players: 2 on a team vs. 1)
Asymmetric rescue scenario. Flavor summary: Sgt. Drake Alexander has been captured; an enchantment will teleport his allies (and him) to safety the moment one of them touches him — but the enchantment is time-limited.

- **Players 1 & 2's Goal (team):** rescue Sgt. Drake Alexander by the end of Round 8.
- **Player 3's Goal:** prevent the rescue.
- **Setup:**
  - 4 glyphs placed power-side up where shown (powers active).
  - 1 additional glyph placed symbol-side up where shown, with the Sgt. Drake Alexander figure standing on top of it (it marks his prison space).
  - Armies: Players 1 and 2 each draft or bring a pre-made **200-point** army; Player 3 may draft or bring a pre-made **500-point** army.
  - Start zones: Players 1 and 2 = navy zone; Player 3 = brown zone.
- **Special Rules:**
  - Drake is scenery, not a combatant: he cannot participate in the battle, cannot be controlled by ANY player, and cannot move from his space.
  - The symbol-side-up glyph under him cannot be moved and its power has no effect on the game.
- **Victory:**
  - Players 1 & 2 win immediately if either of them moves a figure adjacent to ("next to") Drake — that rescues him.
  - Player 3 wins if Drake is not rescued by the end of Round 8 — even if Player 3 has no figures left on the battlefield.
  - Player 3 also wins immediately if Players 1 and 2 have no figures left on the battlefield.

### MASTER GAME — "A Toxic Mist" (2 players)
Attrition scenario with a rising-hazard timer. Flavor summary: a Valkyrie General poisoned the water; the gas slowly fills the valley.

- **Goal:** destroy all of your opponent's figures.
- **Setup:** glyphs placed power-side up where shown; each player drafts or brings a pre-made **400-point** army; one player starts in the brown zone, the other in the navy zone.
- **Special Rules — the rising gas (fully automatic, end-of-round trigger):**

| End of round | Gas coverage |
|---|---|
| 1 | all water spaces + all of level 1 |
| 2 | …plus all of level 2 |
| n | …plus all of level n |
| 5+ | capped — gas never rises above level 5 |

  - At the end of each round, every figure standing in the gas receives **1 wound**.
  - The gas has NO effect during a round — only the end-of-round position matters (figures may pass through gassed spaces freely mid-round).
- **Victory:**
  - You win when the other side has no figures left on the battlefield.
  - Simultaneous mutual wipeout (e.g., both last figures die to the same end-of-round gas tick): NEITHER player wins.
  - Hard time limit: if the game reaches the end of Round 8, it ends and the player with the most points of figures remaining on the battlefield wins (cross-reference: "Scoring," page 14 of the Master Game Guide).

## Page 27 — Migol's Tomb Scenarios (part 1) + build completion

Left column finishes the Migol's Tomb build: **Level 08, Level 09, Level 10, Level 11** diagrams; top-right: **Ruins** diagram. Right margin: **Round Marker Track numbered 1–6**.

### MASTER GAME — "Mimring's Fortress" (4 players, as individuals)
Asymmetric 1-defender-vs-3-raiders scenario with a temporary-truce rule. Flavor summary: a map of 3 enemy wellsprings is being buried in the rock of Barrenspur by a slow device; a dragon guards it until the work is done.

- **Goal:** Player 1 (controlling the dragon Mimring and his minions) must protect the map until it is secure (i.e., survive the round limit). Players 2, 3, and 4 each independently compete to seize the map — they are NOT a team.
- **Setup:**
  - A Glyph of Brandar is placed power-side up where shown; this Artifact glyph represents the map (objective marker).
  - The Glyphs of Astrid, Gerda, Valda, and Mitonsoul are turned symbol-side up, shuffled, and placed on the "?" spaces inside the catacombs (4 random face-down glyphs — their identities are hidden until flipped per normal glyph rules).
  - Mimring's figure is placed where shown on the map (marked position).
  - Armies: Player 1 = Mimring **plus 260 points** of drafted or pre-made armies; Players 2, 3, 4 = **200 points** each.
  - Start zones: Player 1 = brown; Players 2/3/4 each pick from navy, teal, and dark yellow.
  - Diagrams: main map with the four zones and Mimring's start hex labeled, plus a "Catacombs close-up" inset showing the "?" glyph spaces inside the tomb's lower passages.
- **Special Rules — the uneasy alliance (state change, one-way):** Players 2, 3, and 4 begin the game allied — their armies all count as FRIENDLY to one another. The moment any one of them attacks another, the truce is off between ALL of them permanently (it cannot be re-formed). Note this is a player-choice trigger with a global, irreversible effect.
- **Victory:**
  - Checked at end of round: if Player 2, 3, or 4 has a figure on the Artifact glyph at the end of any round, that player takes the map and wins (individual win).
  - If nobody has taken the map by the end of Round 6, Player 1 wins — even if Player 1 has no figures left on the battlefield.

### MASTER GAME — "To Take Barrenspur" (2 players, or 4 players on 2 teams)
Straight battle scenario with a terrain modifier. Flavor summary: rival Valkyrie Generals have each ordered their forces to take Barrenspur and hold Migol's Tomb.

- **Goal:** destroy all of your opponents' figures.
- **Setup:**
  - Glyphs placed power-side up where shown (powers active).
  - Army sizes scale with player count:

| Player count | Army per player |
|---|---|
| 2 players | 400 points each |
| 4 players (2 teams of 2) | 300 points each |

  - Start zones: one player/team = brown zone; the other player/team = navy zone.
  - Diagrams: main map with glyph positions and the two starting zones, plus a "Catacombs close-up" inset.
- **Special Rules — mud (automatic movement modifier):** recent rains have turned all sand spaces to mud; every figure pays **2 movement points per sand space** entered (instead of 1).
- **Victory:**
  - Your side wins when the opposing side has no figures left on the battlefield.
  - Hard time limit: if the game reaches the end of Round 12, the battle ends and the player or team with the most points of figures remaining on the battlefield wins (cross-reference: "Scoring," page 14 of the Master Game Guide).

## Key terms introduced

- **Battlefield build "Level"** — one elevation layer of a terrain build; diagrams add pieces level by level (solid color = new pieces this level, ghosted = already built).
- **Ruins** — vertical ruin-wall scenery placed as the final build step of each battlefield.
- **Round Marker Track** — printed 1–N track on the scenario page where the round marker is advanced; defines each scenario's hard round limit (12 / 8 / 6 / 12 on these pages).
- **Power-side up vs. symbol-side up (glyphs)** — power-side up = glyph identity revealed and (normally) its power active; symbol-side up = face-down/hidden, used here for hidden objectives, decoys, and inert markers.
- **Glyph of Brandar (the "Artifact")** — the blank/no-power glyph, repurposed by scenarios as an objective token (comfrey plant, transfer post, the map).
- **Glyph of Kelda** — used as a hidden-role token (comfrey plant; the secret Hero Scout's marker) in these scenarios.
- **Glyphs of Astrid, Gerda, Valda, Mitonsoul** — the named power glyphs, placed face-up (power active) or face-down as decoys; their powers are defined elsewhere in the rules, not on these pages.
- **Starting zone** — color-coded set of spaces (brown, navy, teal, dark yellow on these maps) where a player's army must be placed.
- **Draft / pre-made army (point budget)** — scenario armies are built to a stated point total (200/260/300/400/500 here) either by drafting or bringing a pre-built force.
- **Hero Scout** — scenario-specific hidden role: the Unique Hero secretly carrying the Glyph of Kelda in "Under Tempest's Cover."
- **Transfer post** — scenario-specific destination space (marked by a Brandar glyph).
- **Comfrey plants** — scenario-specific collectible objectives (Brandar/Kelda glyphs face-down among decoys).
- **"The Drop"** — a special power (parachute deployment) referenced as disabled by the storm rule; defined on its Army Card, not here.
- **Initiative roll modifier** — scenarios can grant a flat bonus (+12 for Round 1 in "Under Tempest's Cover").
- **Uneasy alliance / truce** — scenario state in which several players' armies count as mutually friendly until any one of them attacks another, which permanently breaks the truce for all.
- **Scoring (Master Game Guide p. 14)** — the referenced tiebreak procedure: at a scenario's round limit, the side with the most points of surviving figures on the battlefield wins.
- **Wellspring / Archkyrie / Valkyrie General / Barrenspur / Upper Bleakwoode / Lindesfarme** — setting lore terms used in flavor text (no rules weight).
